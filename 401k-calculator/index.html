How Does a 401(k) Calculator Work?

A 401(k) calculator projects the future value of your employer-sponsored retirement account based on your salary, contribution percentage, employer match, expected investment returns, and time until retirement. The 2026 annual contribution limit is $23,500 for those under 50, with an additional $7,500 catch-up contribution for workers 50 and older.

Understanding Employer Matching

Employer matching is free money. If your employer matches 100% of contributions up to 4% of salary, and you earn $65,000, that's $2,600 per year your employer adds to your account at no cost to you. Not contributing enough to get the full match is leaving money on the table — it's an immediate 100% return on your investment.

How Much Should I Contribute to My 401(k)?

At minimum, contribute enough to capture your full employer match. Beyond that, financial experts recommend saving 15% of gross income for retirement (including employer contributions). If you started late, you may need 20% or more. Use the Retirement Calculator to see if you're on track for your target retirement age.

401(k) vs. Roth IRA

A traditional 401(k) reduces your taxable income now but you pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. A Roth IRA is funded with after-tax dollars but grows and is withdrawn tax-free. Many financial planners recommend contributing to both — 401(k) up to the employer match, then max out a Roth IRA, then contribute more to the 401(k). Workers in states without income tax (Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington) may find the traditional 401(k) slightly more advantageous for reducing federal taxes now.

What Happens When I Change Jobs?

When you leave an employer, you have several options: leave the 401(k) with the old employer, roll it into your new employer's plan, roll it into an IRA, or cash it out (which triggers taxes and a 10% early withdrawal penalty if you're under 59½). Rolling into an IRA typically provides the most investment choices and flexibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I put in my 401k?

Contribute at least enough to get your full employer match. Beyond that, aim for 15% of gross income including employer contributions. The 2026 limit is $23,500 ($31,000 if over 50).

Is a 401k better than a savings account?

For retirement savings, yes. A 401(k) offers tax advantages and employer matching that savings accounts cannot. However, you should still maintain a separate emergency fund in a savings account for short-term needs.

What is a good 401k balance by age?

Common benchmarks: 1x salary by 30, 3x by 40, 6x by 50, 8x by 60. These are rough guidelines — use our calculator with your actual numbers for a personalized projection.

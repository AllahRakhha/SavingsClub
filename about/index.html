Our Mission

SavingsClub exists for a simple reason. Most Americans do not have a financial advisor. Most financial websites feel like they were written for people who already have one. We build free, easy-to-use tools and write educational guides in plain English so anyone can run the numbers on a savings goal, a mortgage decision, a debt payoff plan, or a retirement projection without paying for software or hiring a professional.

Every calculator on this site is free. There is no signup, no email collection, no paywall. Your inputs never leave your browser because we do not store, sell, or share your financial data. The goal is to give you what a financial planner would give you in a first meeting: a clear picture of where you stand and what your numbers actually mean.

Our north star: If a person earning $40,000 per year cannot get clear answers to basic money questions for free, the personal finance industry has failed. SavingsClub is built specifically for that person, not for high net worth individuals who already have advisors and tax professionals.

Why SavingsClub Exists

The American personal finance landscape is filled with paywalls, email gates, and tools that ask you to create accounts before showing basic results. Premium financial calculators cost $300 per year. Financial advisors charge $2,500 to $5,000 for a comprehensive plan. Many free tools collect your data and sell it to marketers.

Meanwhile, the questions most people actually have are mathematical, not personal. How much will my savings grow at 4.5 percent? What is my real mortgage payment? How fast can I pay off this debt with an extra $200 per month? These questions have specific numerical answers. They do not require an advisor. They require a calculator and clear context.

SavingsClub was built to fill this gap. We provide the math, the data, and the educational context that helps everyday Americans answer their own financial questions. For decisions that genuinely require personalized advice (estate planning, complex tax situations, large investments), we consistently point readers toward licensed professionals. For the 90 percent of money questions that are just math, we provide the tools to figure it out yourself.

The Gap We Address

According to the Federal Reserve, only 36 percent of American adults could cover a $400 emergency expense without going into debt. Less than 40 percent of Americans have a written financial plan. Median net worth for households under age 35 is just $39,000. These are not problems caused by lack of intelligence. They are problems made worse by lack of accessible education and tools.

SavingsClub is one small contribution to closing that gap. Free tools. Clear explanations. Transparent methodology. No barriers to entry.

What We Believe

Four principles shape every page, every calculator, and every editorial decision on SavingsClub.

Accessibility Over Authority

Personal finance content should be readable by a high school graduate, not just an MBA. We avoid jargon, define necessary terms, and explain the why behind every recommendation.

Transparency Over Polish

We would rather tell you exactly how our calculators work, where our data comes from, and how we make money than hide it behind marketing copy. Transparency is a feature, not a flaw.

Free Tools Stay Free

Every calculator on SavingsClub is free and will remain free. We will never add a premium tier, paywall basic features, or require accounts to access tools.

Privacy Is the Default

Your financial data is yours. Our calculators run entirely in your browser. We do not collect inputs. We do not require accounts. We do not sell visitor data to anyone.

Who Runs SavingsClub

SavingsClub is an independently operated personal finance website. We are not a publishing company, a financial institution, or a venture backed startup. We are a small team focused on building useful tools and producing accessible educational content for people who want clear answers about money.

We do not have a board of certified financial planners. We have not raised investor capital. We have deliberately chosen not to present ourselves as something we are not. What we do have is a commitment to accuracy, honest disclosures, and making sure every visitor walks away with information they can actually use.

The SavingsClub Research Team

All content on SavingsClub is published under the byline "SavingsClub Research Team." This reflects our actual workflow: educational content is researched, drafted with the help of AI tools, then reviewed by a human before publication. We have chosen team attribution rather than individual bylines for three reasons:

  • Honesty about the process: Our content is a collaboration between AI research tools and human review, not solo author work. Team attribution reflects this accurately.
  • Consistency of voice: Every piece of content goes through the same editorial process and meets the same standards, regardless of who reviews it.
  • Privacy in a small operation: We are a small team. Team attribution lets us focus on content quality rather than personal branding.

How Our Content Is Created

We believe in being completely upfront about how our content is produced. Our educational guides and articles are researched and written with the help of modern AI research tools, then reviewed before publication to verify accuracy, clarity, and adherence to our editorial standards.

This approach is increasingly common across major publishers. Forbes, USA Today, the Associated Press, and many other established media organizations openly disclose their use of AI tools in content production. We follow the same transparent practice.

Why We Use AI Tools

AI tools allow a small independent operation to produce more accurate, more current content than would otherwise be possible. They help us research topics quickly, structure educational explanations clearly, and maintain a consistent publication schedule. They do not replace human judgment, fact-checking, or accountability.

What AI Does Not Do

AI does not invent statistics, fabricate sources, or pretend our content was written by licensed professionals. When we cite numbers, rules, or rates, we direct readers to the original authoritative sources (government agencies, FDIC, IRS publications, Federal Reserve data, established financial institutions) so readers can verify everything independently.

The Human Review Step

Every piece of content on SavingsClub is reviewed by a human before publication. This includes verifying calculations, checking source citations, confirming data accuracy, and ensuring the content reflects current laws, contribution limits, and best practices. If you ever find an error, the responsibility is ours.

Read more: For complete details on our AI usage policy, fact-checking process, and editorial standards, see our Editorial Standards page.

Our Editorial Commitments

We make these specific commitments to every reader who visits SavingsClub.

  • We will not lie to you about our process. When AI tools are involved in content creation, we disclose it. When we have affiliate relationships, we disclose them. When we do not know something, we say so.
  • We will not invent expertise we do not have. We are not licensed financial advisors and we will never claim to be. We provide educational information, not personalized advice.
  • We will not fabricate statistics or sources. Every numerical claim on SavingsClub is traceable to a primary source (government agency, regulatory body, or established research organization).
  • We will not bury affiliate relationships. Every sponsored placement is clearly labeled, includes an advertiser disclosure, and uses FTC-compliant link attributes (target="_top" and rel="sponsored nofollow").
  • We will not paywall basic information. All calculators remain free. All educational content remains free. There will never be a premium tier locked behind payment.
  • We will fix errors quickly. When we discover or are notified of an error, we update the affected page within 48 hours and add a visible correction notice for substantive errors.
  • We will keep our process documented. Our editorial standards, methodology, and disclosure practices are publicly documented on this site for any reader to review.

What SavingsClub Is Not

We want to be specific about our limitations because too many finance websites blur these lines. Reading what we are NOT is just as important as reading what we are.

  1. Not Licensed Financial Advisors

    No one on our team is a Certified Financial Planner (CFP), Certified Public Accountant (CPA), attorney, registered investment advisor, or licensed insurance professional. Our content is educational, not advisory.

  2. Not a Source of Personalized Advice

    Our content cannot account for your specific situation, goals, risk tolerance, tax position, or family circumstances. General educational information cannot replace personalized advice from a qualified licensed professional.

  3. Not Affiliated with Banks, Lenders, or Card Issuers

    We have no insider knowledge of any financial institution. When we discuss specific companies, it is based on publicly available information. We are not employees, agents, or partners of any bank or lender.

  4. Not a Substitute for Professional Services

    For decisions involving large sums, complex taxes, estate planning, complex investments, or legal matters, please consult a qualified licensed professional. Our content is a starting point for self-education, not a replacement for personalized professional services.

  5. Not a Tax or Legal Authority

    While we cite IRS rules and tax brackets, we are not tax professionals. Tax law is complex and personal. For tax decisions specific to your situation, consult a CPA, Enrolled Agent, or tax attorney.

  6. Not a Crystal Ball

    Our calculators show projections based on assumptions. They do not predict markets, guarantee returns, or forecast specific outcomes. Future market performance, inflation, and tax law are unpredictable.

Saying this clearly is more useful to you than pretending we are something we are not. We would rather be honest about our limitations than overstate our authority.

How We Make Money

SavingsClub is free to use and we intend to keep it that way. We support the site through affiliate partnerships with financial product providers. When you click certain links on our site and decide to apply for a product like a credit monitoring service, savings account, or credit card, we may receive a commission from the provider at no additional cost to you.

How Affiliate Disclosures Work on SavingsClub

Every affiliate placement on this site is clearly marked with a "Sponsored" label, includes an advertiser disclosure paragraph, and uses standard FTC-compliant link attributes (target="_top" and rel="sponsored nofollow"). You will always know when you are looking at a partnership versus general educational content.

What We Will Not Do

  • We do not accept payment to write positive reviews
  • We do not claim products are "the best" without context
  • We do not hide downsides to maximize commissions
  • We do not promote products we would not recommend to a family member
  • We do not present sponsored content as objective comparisons

How We Choose Affiliate Partners

When evaluating potential affiliate partners, we ask:

  • Does this product solve a real problem readers face?
  • Are the pricing and features clearly disclosed by the provider?
  • Does the provider have a legitimate business model?
  • Are there meaningful free alternatives we should discuss alongside this product?
  • Are there downsides we should mention alongside benefits?

We are a small operation, so our list of affiliate partners is currently limited. We are actively evaluating additional partnerships and will add them as we form them, always with the same transparency standards. Our full affiliate disclosure is available on our disclaimer page.

Our Approach to Recommendations

When we mention specific financial products or providers, we aim to be educational rather than promotional. We explain how a product category works, what features matter most, what to watch out for, and how to evaluate options against your own situation.

We avoid making absolute claims like "the best credit card" or "the number one savings account" because the best product depends entirely on the individual. The best 401(k) strategy for someone earning $40,000 is different from the best for someone earning $200,000. The best credit card for a frequent traveler is wrong for someone trying to build credit. Educational content respects that nuance.

What You Will Find in Our Reviews

  • Clear explanation of who the product is appropriate for
  • Honest discussion of limitations and downsides
  • Comparison against free alternatives where they exist
  • Source-cited claims about features and pricing
  • Clear disclosure of any affiliate relationship

What You Will Not Find

  • Vague "this is amazing" claims without specific reasoning
  • Hidden downsides or omitted limitations
  • Claims that compare unfairly to alternatives
  • Fabricated user testimonials
  • Pressure tactics or artificial urgency

Q&A

What is SavingsClub's mission and who is it for?

Short answer: SavingsClub exists to give everyday Americans (especially those without advisors) free, plain-English tools and guides to answer real money questions with math, not marketing. The site's "north star" is that someone earning about $40,000 per year should be able to get clear answers to basic finance questions for free. Everything is designed around four principles: Accessibility over authority (no jargon; simple explanations), Transparency over polish (clear methods, sources, and disclosures), Free tools stay free (no paywalls or premium tiers), and Privacy by default (no accounts; no data collection from calculator inputs).

Do I need an account, and what happens to my data when I use the calculators?

Short answer: You never need an account, email, or signup to use SavingsClub. Every calculator runs entirely in your browser, so the numbers you enter never leave your device and aren't stored or sold. The site uses Google Analytics only for anonymous, aggregated metrics like page views, country, and device type that cannot be linked to your calculator inputs. Cookies are minimal (e.g., remembering you dismissed the cookie banner and basic analytics); there are no advertising tracking cookies. See the Privacy Policy and Cookie Policy for full details.

If everything is free, how does SavingsClub make money and are recommendations unbiased?

Short answer: The site earns revenue through affiliate partnerships. If you click a clearly labeled "Sponsored" link and apply for a product (e.g., a credit monitoring service or savings account), SavingsClub may receive a commission at no extra cost to you. All affiliate placements include an advertiser disclosure and FTC-compliant link attributes (target="_top" and rel="sponsored nofollow"). The team's guardrails are explicit: no paid-for positive reviews, no "best" claims without context, no hiding downsides, no pressure tactics, and no sponsored content presented as objective comparisons. Reviews explain who a product fits, tradeoffs, free alternatives, and source-cited features/pricing.

Are you financial advisors? Can you give me personalized advice?

Short answer: No. SavingsClub is not a licensed advisor (not CFPs, CPAs, attorneys, RIAs, or insurance professionals) and provides educational information, not personalized advice. The calculators and guides help you run the numbers and understand tradeoffs, but they don't predict markets or guarantee outcomes. For decisions involving large sums, complex taxes, estate planning, complex investments, or legal issues, you should consult qualified licensed professionals.

How is content created and kept accurate if you use AI tools?

Short answer: Educational content is researched and drafted with the help of AI tools, then reviewed by a human before publication. Human review covers calculations, source citations, data accuracy, and current rules (e.g., IRS limits). Numerical claims are traceable to primary sources such as government agencies and regulators. Errors are corrected quickly. Substantive fixes are targeted within 48 hours with a visible correction notice. The site's methodology and editorial standards are publicly documented. Note that SavingsClub is a young site and not a substitute for personalized professional advice, so readers should verify key decisions against authoritative sources.

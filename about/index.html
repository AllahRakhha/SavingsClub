About SavingsClub

An independent personal finance resource offering free calculators and clear, accessible educational content for everyday Americans.

Our Mission

SavingsClub exists for a simple reason: most people don't have a financial advisor, and most financial websites feel like they were written for people who already have one. We build free, easy-to-use tools and write educational guides in plain English so anyone can run the numbers on a savings goal, a mortgage decision, a debt payoff plan, or a retirement projection without paying for software or hiring a professional.

Every calculator on this site is free. There's no signup, no email collection, no paywall. Your inputs never leave your browser we don't store, sell, or share your financial data. The goal is to give you what a financial planner would give you in their first meeting: a clear picture of where you stand and what your numbers actually mean.

Who Runs SavingsClub

SavingsClub is an independently operated personal finance website. We're not a publishing company, a financial institution, or a venture-backed startup. We're a small operation focused on building useful tools and producing accessible educational content for people who want straight answers about money.

We don't have a board of financial advisors or a staff of certified planners. We've deliberately chosen not to present ourselves as something we're not. What we do have is a commitment to accuracy, to honest disclosures, and to making sure every visitor walks away with information they can actually use.

How Our Content Is Created

We believe in being upfront about how our content is produced. Our educational guides and articles are researched and written with the help of modern AI research tools, then reviewed before publication to verify accuracy, clarity, and tone. This is the same approach used by many major publishers today, including Forbes, USA Today, and the Associated Press, who openly disclose their use of AI assistance in content production.

We use AI tools to research topics quickly, structure educational explanations clearly, and produce content at a pace a small independent operation otherwise couldn't match. We do not use AI to fabricate statistics, invent expert quotes, or pretend our content was written by licensed professionals. When we cite numbers, rules, or rates, we point you to authoritative sources — government agencies, FDIC, IRS publications, and reputable financial institutions — so you can verify everything yourself.

Our calculators are built using standard financial formulas the same compound interest, amortization, and time-value-of-money equations taught in finance classes. The math is the math. Where assumptions are required (inflation rates, tax brackets, market returns), we use widely accepted default values and tell you what those defaults are.

What SavingsClub Is Not

We want to be specific about our limitations, because too many finance websites blur these lines:

  • We are not licensed financial advisors. No one on our team is a CFP, CPA, attorney, registered investment advisor, or licensed insurance professional.
  • We do not provide personalized financial advice. Our content is educational. It cannot account for your specific situation, goals, risk tolerance, tax position, or family circumstances.
  • We are not affiliated with banks, lenders, or card issuers. We have no insider knowledge of any financial institution. When we discuss specific companies, it is based on publicly available information.
  • We are not a substitute for professional advice. For decisions involving large sums, tax implications, estate planning, or complex investments, please consult a qualified licensed professional.

Saying this clearly is more useful to you than pretending we're something we're not.

How We Make Money

SavingsClub is free to use, and we intend to keep it that way. We support the site through affiliate partnerships with financial product providers. When you click certain links on our site and decide to apply for a product like a credit monitoring service, savings account, or credit card we may receive a commission from the provider at no additional cost to you.

Every affiliate placement on this site is clearly marked with a "Sponsored" label, includes an advertiser disclosure, and uses standard FTC-compliant link attributes. You'll always know when you're looking at a partnership versus general educational content.

We do not accept payment to write positive reviews. We do not claim products are "the best" without context. When we discuss financial products, we try to explain who they're appropriate for and where their limitations are. We're a small operation, so our list of affiliate partners is currently limited we're actively evaluating additional partnerships and will add them as we form them. Our full affiliate disclosure is available on our disclaimer page.

Our Approach to Recommendations

When we mention specific financial products or providers, we aim to be educational rather than promotional. We try to explain how a product category works, what features matter most, what to watch out for, and how to evaluate options against your own situation. We avoid making absolute claims like "the best credit card" or "the #1 savings account," because the best product depends entirely on the individual. The "best" 401(k) strategy for someone earning $40,000 is different from the "best" for someone earning $200,000. The "best" credit card for a frequent traveler is wrong for someone trying to build credit. Educational content respects that nuance.

Why You Should (and Shouldn't) Trust Us

Reasons to trust the information here: Our calculators use standard financial math. Our educational content is based on publicly verifiable sources. We disclose how content is produced. We disclose how we make money. We say what we don't know.

Reasons not to trust us blindly: We're a young website. We're not licensed professionals. We can't see your full financial picture. Even with the best research, mistakes are possible. For any significant financial decision, please verify our information against authoritative sources and consult a qualified professional. We're meant to be a useful starting point not the last word.

Privacy

Your privacy matters. Every calculator on SavingsClub runs entirely in your browser. The numbers you enter your salary, your debts, your savings, your retirement balance never leave your device and are never sent to our servers. We don't have an account system, we don't ask for your email, and we don't collect personal financial information. For details, see our Privacy Policy.

Get in Touch

If you spot a mistake incorrect math, outdated information, a broken link, anything please let us know. We'd rather fix something fast than leave bad information up.

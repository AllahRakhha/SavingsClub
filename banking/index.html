How to Choose the Right Bank Account

Choosing the right bank account depends on how you use your money day-to-day. If you keep a significant balance in savings, prioritize APY the difference between 0.40% and 4.40% on $20,000 is $800/year. If you primarily need a spending account, focus on fee structure, ATM access, and direct deposit timing.

Online Banks vs. Traditional Banks

Online-only banks consistently offer higher APYs because they have lower overhead costs — no branches, fewer employees. The trade-off is no in-person service. For most Americans, especially those comfortable with mobile banking, online banks provide significantly better value. All banks listed on this page are FDIC insured, meaning your deposits are protected up to $250,000 regardless of whether the bank has a physical location.

FDIC Insurance: Is My Money Safe?

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation insures deposits at member banks up to $250,000 per depositor, per institution. Joint accounts are insured up to $500,000. In the FDIC's history, no depositor has lost a single penny of insured deposits. If you have more than $250,000, spread deposits across multiple FDIC-insured banks.

Understanding APY vs. Interest Rate

APY (Annual Percentage Yield) includes the effect of compounding, while a simple interest rate does not. A 4.40% APY means you earn slightly more than 4.40% simple interest because your earned interest also earns interest. When comparing savings accounts, always compare APY to APY. Use our Compound Interest Calculator to see exactly how your deposits grow over time.

How Many Bank Accounts Should I Have?

A solid setup for most people includes one checking account for daily spending, one high-yield savings for your emergency fund, and optionally a second savings account for specific goals (down payment, vacation). More than that creates complexity without meaningful benefit. Use our Budget Planner to determine how much should flow into each account monthly.

Credit Score Explained: What It Is and How to Improve It

by SavingsClub Research Team · January 25, 2026

Information reflects conditions as of early 2026.

Your Three-Digit Financial Reputation

Your credit score (300-850) affects mortgage rates, credit card approvals, apartment rentals, and even insurance premiums. Above 740 gets the best rates; below 620 means limited options.

How It’s Calculated

Payment history (35%), credit utilization (30%), length of history (15%), credit mix (10%), and new inquiries (10%). The two biggest factors — payment history and utilization — are also the most controllable.

Quick Wins

Pay every bill on time. Keep credit card balances below 30% of limits (under 10% is ideal). Don’t close old accounts. Check reports for errors at AnnualCreditReport.com. Request credit limit increases without spending more. Americans in every state from New Jersey to Oregon can implement these strategies immediately.

How Does a CD Calculator Work?

A CD (Certificate of Deposit) calculator shows how much your deposit will earn by maturity based on the APY and term length. CDs offer guaranteed returns because the interest rate is locked for the full term. Unlike savings accounts, early withdrawal typically incurs a penalty.

Current CD Rates in 2026

Top online banks offer CD rates between 4.00-4.75% APY for 12-month terms. Shorter terms (3-6 months) may offer slightly lower rates, while longer terms (3-5 years) vary based on the interest rate environment. Compare CD rates alongside high-yield savings accounts to find the best option for your timeline.

CD vs. High-Yield Savings Account

CDs lock in a guaranteed rate but restrict access to your money. High-yield savings accounts offer flexibility but rates can change at any time. If you won't need the money for a specific period, a CD may earn slightly more. If you need liquidity, a savings account is better. Both are FDIC insured up to $250,000.

CD Laddering Strategy

Instead of putting all your money in one CD, spread it across multiple CDs with staggered maturity dates (3 months, 6 months, 12 months, etc.). As each CD matures, reinvest in the longest term. This gives you periodic access to funds while capturing higher long-term rates. Use our Savings Calculator to compare CD returns with regular savings growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are CDs worth it in 2026?

With rates at 4-4.75% APY, CDs are competitive if you can lock up funds for the full term. They are best for money you won't need within the CD term and want a guaranteed return.

What happens if I withdraw a CD early?

Most banks charge an early withdrawal penalty, typically 3-12 months of interest depending on the CD term. Some banks offer no-penalty CDs with slightly lower rates.

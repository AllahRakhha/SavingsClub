How to Use This Calculator

Follow these steps to see exactly how your money will grow over time.

  1. Enter your starting principalThis is the amount you already have saved or are starting with. If you're starting from zero, enter 0.
  2. Add your monthly contributionHow much you plan to invest each month going forward. Even $50 per month adds up significantly over time.
  3. Set the annual interest rateFor high-yield savings: 4-5%. For CDs: 4-5.5%. For stock market index funds: historical average is 7-10% annually.
  4. Choose your time horizonHow many years you'll let the money grow. The longer the period, the more dramatic the compound effect.
  5. Pick your compounding frequencyMost savings accounts compound daily; investments typically compound monthly or based on returns. Daily yields slightly more than monthly.
  6. (Optional) Show Advanced OptionsClick to add yearly contribution increases (e.g., raises), inflation rate to see real purchasing power, and tax rate to see after-tax results.
  7. Review the year-by-year tableSwitch between yearly and monthly views to see exactly how interest accumulates each period.

The Power of Compound Interest

Compound interest has been called the eighth wonder of the world. It means earning returns on your returns and it's the single most powerful force in wealth building. A $10,000 investment growing at 7% annually becomes $76,122 in 30 years without adding a single dollar.

Why Starting Early Matters

Starting at 25 instead of 35 with $500/month at 7% annual returns means the difference between $1.2 million and $567,000 at age 65. That 10-year head start more than doubles the outcome not because of extra contributions, but because of compound growth.

Compounding Frequency Explained

The more frequently interest compounds, the more you earn. Daily compounding produces slightly more than monthly, which produces more than annual. The difference is small over short periods but adds up over decades.

Why Inflation Matters

A million dollars in 30 years won't have the same purchasing power as a million dollars today. Use the inflation rate option to see your investment's "real" value in today's dollars typically 2-3% inflation is a reasonable assumption.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is compound interest?

Compound interest is interest earned on both your original principal and previously earned interest. Unlike simple interest, which only calculates returns on the original amount, compound interest creates an exponential growth effect over time.

How often should interest compound?

More frequent compounding produces slightly higher returns. Daily compounding yields slightly more than monthly, which yields more than annual. Most savings accounts compound daily, while investments typically compound based on market returns.

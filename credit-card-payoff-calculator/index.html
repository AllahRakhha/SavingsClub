The Minimum Payment Trap

Paying only the minimum on a $5,000 balance at 22% APR takes over 20 years and costs more than $8,000 in interest. By paying $200/month instead, you're done in about 2.5 years and save thousands. This calculator shows you the exact difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are credit card minimum payments calculated?

Most issuers set the minimum at 2% of the balance or $25, whichever is greater. On a $5,000 balance, that's $100 — barely covering interest at typical APRs.

Our Editorial Mission

SavingsClub exists to make personal finance simpler for everyday Americans. We believe financial knowledge should be accessible without paywalls, signups, or sales pressure. Every calculator, guide, and blog post on this site is researched, written, and reviewed with one goal: help readers make informed money decisions.

We are not licensed financial advisors. We do not provide personalized financial advice. What we offer is clear educational content based on credible sources, accurate calculations using standard financial formulas, and transparent explanations of how money concepts actually work.

This page explains exactly how our content is created, where our information comes from, and what standards we hold ourselves to. Transparency is not optional in personal finance content. It is the foundation of trust.

Core Editorial Principles

Every piece of content on SavingsClub follows these five non-negotiable principles.

  1. Accuracy Over Speed

    We would rather publish slower than publish inaccurate financial information. Every calculation, statistic, and claim is verified against primary sources before publication. When data is uncertain or estimates vary, we say so clearly.

  2. Sources Over Opinions

    Personal finance is not opinion. It is math, regulations, and verified data. We cite the Federal Reserve, FTC, FDIC, IRS, Department of Labor, and other authoritative sources rather than rely on our own assertions. When we share a perspective, we identify it clearly as such.

  3. Educational, Not Advisory

    SavingsClub provides educational content. We do not provide personalized financial advice. Every page makes this distinction clear. For decisions involving your specific situation, we consistently direct readers to consult licensed financial advisors, tax professionals, or legal counsel.

  4. Transparent Disclosures

    We disclose all affiliate relationships, AI tool usage, and limitations of our content. Readers should know exactly what we are and are not. Every page has a disclaimer. Every affiliate link is labeled "Sponsored" with full advertiser disclosure. No exceptions.

  5. Reader Benefit First

    When affiliate revenue conflicts with reader benefit, the reader wins. We do not promote products we would not recommend to a family member. We disclose downsides alongside benefits. We compare paid products against free alternatives so readers can choose what actually fits their situation.

AI Usage Disclosure

How We Use AI Tools

SavingsClub uses AI tools, including large language models, as part of our content research and drafting process. We believe in transparent disclosure of this practice rather than hiding it.

What AI helps us do: Research summarization, initial draft writing, source identification, content structuring, and editorial refinement.

What AI does NOT do: Replace human judgment, bypass fact-checking, generate citations without verification, or publish content without human review.

Every page on SavingsClub is reviewed by a human before publication. This includes verifying calculations, checking source citations, confirming data accuracy, and ensuring the content reflects current laws, rates, and best practices.

We use AI tools because they make personal finance education more accessible. A solo team using AI responsibly can produce more accurate, more current content than a large editorial team without AI. The key word is "responsibly." AI is a research and drafting assistant. It is not a replacement for human judgment, fact-checking, or accountability.

If you ever find an error in our content, the responsibility is ours, not the AI's. We own every piece of content we publish, regardless of which tools helped create it.

Our Research Process

Every piece of educational content on SavingsClub follows this five-step research and review process.

  1. Topic Selection and Reader Research

    Topics are selected based on real questions Americans are asking about personal finance. We use keyword research tools, reader feedback, and analysis of common financial concerns to identify topics that genuinely help readers, not just topics that drive clicks.

  2. Source Gathering and Verification

    Before writing begins, we gather data from authoritative primary sources. For statistics, we cite the Federal Reserve, Bureau of Labor Statistics, Census Bureau, or relevant federal agencies. For regulations, we cite the IRS, SEC, FDIC, or applicable governing body. Secondary sources like established financial publications are used only when primary sources do not address a topic directly.

  3. Draft Creation with AI Assistance

    Initial drafts are created with AI assistance for structure and clarity. The AI tools we use do not have access to live data or browse the web during drafting. All factual claims must be independently verified by a human editor before publication.

  4. Human Review and Fact-Checking

    Every draft is reviewed by a human editor who verifies all numbers, dates, percentages, regulations, and claims against the original sources. Calculation logic is tested against known inputs and expected outputs. Any claim that cannot be verified is removed or rewritten with appropriate uncertainty language.

  5. Publication and Ongoing Review

    After publication, content is added to a review queue. Pages are revisited periodically to verify continued accuracy. Tax brackets, contribution limits, interest rates, and other time-sensitive data are reviewed when authoritative sources publish updates.

Trusted Sources We Cite

The credibility of any personal finance content depends entirely on the credibility of its sources. SavingsClub relies on primary sources from federal agencies, government statistical bureaus, and established financial regulators. Below are the sources we cite most frequently.

Federal Reserve System

Primary source for interest rates, monetary policy, household financial data, and Survey of Consumer Finances statistics.

FRED (St. Louis Fed)

Federal Reserve Economic Data for mortgage rates, inflation (CPI), personal savings rate, and Fed Funds Rate historical data.

Federal Trade Commission

Source for identity theft statistics, consumer fraud data, credit report dispute regulations, and consumer protection guidelines.

FDIC

Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation for deposit insurance limits, bank failure data, and banking regulation references.

Internal Revenue Service

Tax brackets, retirement account contribution limits, deduction rules, and federal tax regulations.

Bureau of Labor Statistics

Wage data, employment statistics, inflation measures, and household spending patterns from the Consumer Expenditure Survey.

CFPB

Consumer Financial Protection Bureau for consumer protection rules, mortgage regulations, debt collection laws, and complaint data.

SEC

Securities and Exchange Commission for investment regulations, broker-dealer rules, mutual fund disclosures, and investor protection.

Why primary sources matter: Many personal finance websites cite each other in circles, repeating outdated or incorrect information without verification. We cite original government and regulatory sources whenever possible to give readers a direct line to authoritative data they can verify themselves.

Fact-Checking Standards

Personal finance content is only as valuable as it is accurate. SavingsClub applies these fact-checking standards to every piece of content before publication.

Statistical Claims

Any statistic on SavingsClub (such as "the median American net worth is $192,900") must be traceable to a primary source published within the last 24 months. Older data is permitted only when current data does not exist, and the publication date is clearly noted.

Mathematical Claims

Calculations on calculator pages use standard financial formulas (present value, future value, amortization, compound interest). Results are verified against known reference cases. For example, our compound interest calculator results match calculations produced by major financial institutions when given identical inputs.

Regulatory Claims

Any claim about tax law, retirement account rules, banking regulations, or consumer protection rules must cite the relevant federal agency, IRS publication, or current regulatory document. Effective dates are noted when rules have changed recently.

Pricing and Feature Claims

For affiliate products, we describe pricing and features based on the provider's current public marketing materials. We include a disclaimer that pricing and features may change and readers should verify directly with the provider before subscribing.

What We Do Not Claim

SavingsClub does not claim to predict markets, guarantee investment returns, or promise specific outcomes from following our content. Anyone making such claims about personal finance is either misinformed or selling something. We are neither.

Calculator Methodology

SavingsClub offers 17 free financial calculators covering savings, mortgages, retirement, debt payoff, and other common money decisions. All calculators use standard financial formulas commonly used in financial planning software, online tools, and educational materials.

Formula Sources

Our calculators implement formulas documented in standard financial textbooks and used by major financial institutions. Examples include:

  • Compound Interest: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) for principal compounding, with periodic contributions added using the future value annuity formula
  • Mortgage Payment: Standard amortization formula M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n - 1]
  • Net Worth: Total Assets minus Total Liabilities, with separate calculations for liquid and investable net worth
  • Debt Payoff: Standard avalanche and snowball methodologies based on interest rate priority or smallest balance priority

Default Values and Assumptions

Calculator default values use realistic averages from current Federal Reserve, BLS, or industry data. For example, our retirement calculator uses an 8 percent default annual return based on the long-term S&P 500 average, with notes that actual returns vary and recent returns may differ significantly from long-term averages.

Limitations of Calculator Results

All calculator results are estimates. They do not account for taxes, fees, market volatility, inflation, life events, or personal circumstances unless explicitly noted. Results are intended for educational planning, not as binding financial projections. Readers making major financial decisions should consult licensed professionals.

Affiliate Disclosure Policy

SavingsClub earns revenue through affiliate partnerships. When a reader signs up for a financial product through one of our affiliate links, we may receive a commission at no additional cost to the reader. Here is exactly how we handle this.

What We Disclose

Every affiliate link on SavingsClub is labeled "Sponsored" and includes an advertiser disclosure paragraph nearby. Every page that contains affiliate content includes a disclaimer at the bottom. Our full disclaimer page explains the relationship in detail.

How We Choose Affiliate Partners

We only feature affiliate products that we would recommend to family members. Specifically, we evaluate:

  • Does the product solve a real problem readers face?
  • Are the pricing and features clearly disclosed by the provider?
  • Does the provider have a legitimate business model?
  • Are there meaningful free alternatives we should also discuss?
  • Are there downsides we should mention alongside benefits?

What We Do Not Do

We do not promote products we would not use ourselves. We do not hide downsides to maximize commissions. We do not present sponsored content as objective comparisons. We do not claim "best" status on products we have not researched against alternatives.

Reader Cost

Affiliate commissions are paid by the product provider, not by readers. You pay the same price whether you use our affiliate link or go directly to the provider's website. Our affiliate relationships keep SavingsClub free for everyone.

Content Updates and Reviews

Personal finance content goes stale faster than most content categories. Interest rates change. Tax brackets adjust annually. Contribution limits update. Regulations evolve. We maintain a regular review cycle to keep content accurate.

Annual Reviews

Every January, we review and update all content related to tax law, retirement account contribution limits, IRA rules, 401(k) limits, and Social Security data. Year-references are updated, dollar amounts are adjusted to current values, and any expired information is removed or noted.

Quarterly Reviews

Mortgage rates, savings rates, and inflation-sensitive content are reviewed quarterly. Calculator default values are adjusted to reflect current averages.

Triggered Updates

When the Federal Reserve changes interest rates, when major tax legislation passes, or when significant regulatory changes occur, affected pages are updated within 30 days. The "last updated" date on each page reflects the most recent substantive review.

Archive Policy

Older blog posts are kept available for historical reference but include date stamps clearly showing when they were written. We do not silently update old posts to make outdated information appear current. When we update a page substantively, we update the publication date.

Corrections and Feedback

We make mistakes. When we do, we correct them promptly and transparently.

Found an Error? Tell Us.

If you find a factual error, a calculation mistake, an outdated number, or a misleading statement on SavingsClub, please email us at info@savingsclub.com. We respond to corrections quickly because accuracy is foundational to our mission.

How We Handle Corrections

When a correction is needed, we update the affected page immediately and add a note at the top or bottom indicating what was changed and when. For minor corrections (typos, formatting), we update without notice. For substantive corrections (incorrect data, math errors, misleading claims), we add a visible correction notice.

How We Welcome Feedback

Beyond corrections, we welcome general feedback on our content. If a calculator is confusing, if a guide leaves out important context, or if you have a personal finance topic you wish we covered, let us know through our contact page. Reader feedback shapes what we publish next.

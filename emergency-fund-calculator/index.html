Building Your Safety Net

An emergency fund covers unexpected expenses so you don't go into debt. Experts recommend 3-6 months of essential expenses. Start with a $500 mini-fund, then build to $1,000, then one month, then three months. Automate transfers — even $50/week builds to $2,600 per year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should my emergency fund be in a savings account?

Yes. Keep your emergency fund in a high-yield savings account for easy access and competitive interest. Do not invest it in stocks — you need it liquid and stable for true emergencies.

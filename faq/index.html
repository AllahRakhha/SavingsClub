Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SavingsClub, our calculators, and how we help Americans manage money.

🏠 About SavingsClub
What is SavingsClub?▾

SavingsClub is a free personal finance website built for Americans across all 50 states. We offer 17 financial calculators covering savings, budgeting, debt, retirement, mortgages, and more — plus expert-written money guides published three times a week, credit card comparisons, and curated banking recommendations. Everything is 100% free with no account required.

Is SavingsClub really free?▾

Yes, completely free. Every calculator, guide, quiz, and comparison tool is available without creating an account, entering an email address, or paying anything. There are no paywalls, no premium tiers, and no hidden fees. We cover operating costs through affiliate partnerships — see "How does SavingsClub remain free?" below.

Is my financial data safe?▾

Yes. All calculations run entirely in your browser using JavaScript. The numbers you enter into our calculators never leave your device — they are not transmitted to our servers, stored in any database, or shared with anyone. We use HTTPS encryption on every page and implement Content Security Policy headers for additional protection. You can read more in our Privacy Policy.

How does SavingsClub remain free?▾

We earn referral fees when visitors choose to explore financial products — such as savings accounts, credit cards, or checking accounts — through our partner links. These partnerships allow us to cover operating costs and continue building free tools. Importantly, our calculators function identically regardless of any partnerships, and our editorial recommendations are based solely on what we believe serves readers best. Full details are in our Affiliate Disclosure.

🔢 Calculators
How accurate are the calculators?▾

Our calculators use standard financial formulas (compound interest, amortization schedules, tax bracket calculations) and are reliable for planning and estimation purposes. Actual results may vary due to rate changes, fees, market conditions, and individual circumstances. We recommend verifying important financial decisions with your bank, financial advisor, or tax professional.

Do I need an account to use the calculators?▾

No. Every calculator is available immediately — just visit the page and start entering your numbers. No signup, no email, no login. Your data stays in your browser and disappears when you close the page.

Which calculator should I use first?▾

Start with the Budget Planner to understand your 50/30/20 breakdown. Then use the Savings Calculator to see how your savings grow over time. If you have debt, the Debt Payoff Calculator helps you create a payoff plan. For retirement, try our 401(k) Calculator or Retirement Calculator.

What calculators does SavingsClub offer?▾

We offer 17 free calculators: Savings, Budget Planner, Debt Payoff, Compound Interest, Retirement, Investment, Mortgage, Auto Loan, Net Worth, Emergency Fund, Credit Card Payoff, 401(k), Roth IRA, Inflation, Loan Payoff, Paycheck/Take-Home Pay, and CD Calculator. Plus two quizzes: Financial Health Score and Credit Card Quiz.

🏦 Banking & Credit Cards
How do you choose which banks to recommend?▾

We evaluate banks based on APY rates, fee structures, minimum balance requirements, FDIC insurance, mobile app quality, ATM network size, and customer service reputation. We only feature FDIC-insured institutions. While some recommended banks are affiliate partners, we apply the same evaluation criteria to all institutions. Visit our Banking page to see current recommendations.

Are the recommended savings accounts FDIC insured?▾

Yes, without exception. Every bank and savings account featured on SavingsClub is FDIC insured, which means your deposits are protected up to $250,000 per depositor, per institution. Joint accounts are insured up to $500,000. In the FDIC's history, no depositor has ever lost a penny of insured funds.

How often are rates updated?▾

We review and update banking rates and credit card information regularly. However, rates can change at any time without notice. We always recommend verifying the current rate directly with the financial institution before opening an account. All rates on our site reflect conditions as of the most recent review.

Do you recommend credit cards based on commissions?▾

No. Our credit card recommendations are based on reward rates, annual fees, APR, sign-up bonuses, and suitability for different spending patterns. While we may earn referral fees when you apply through our links, this does not influence which cards we recommend or how we rank them. Our Credit Card Quiz matches you with cards based on your actual spending habits.

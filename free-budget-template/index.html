A personal budget spreadsheet template that does the work for you

Most free budget templates are just an empty grid with the word "Budget" at the top. You still have to build the formulas, decide the categories, and work out whether your numbers are actually good or bad. That is why so many of them get downloaded once and never opened again.

This one is different in a way that matters. The math is finished. Every total, every percentage, and every color is calculated the moment you type a number, so the spreadsheet tells you what is wrong instead of waiting for you to notice.

Red and green tell you the storyGo over budget on a line and it turns red. Come in under and it turns green. Hit your savings target and the dashboard turns green too. You never have to hunt for the problem.
A dashboard, not just a listYour income, money out, what is left, your savings rate, and your full 50/30/20 breakdown sit on one screen and update on their own.
Plan versus reality, side by sideEach line has a Budgeted column and an Actual column. The gap between those two numbers is usually the whole reason a budget was not working.

What is inside the budget template

The workbook has three tabs, and you only ever type into one of them:

  • Start Here. A plain-English guide to the layout. About thirty seconds of reading.
  • Monthly Budget. Income, fixed expenses, variable expenses, savings and goals, and debt payoff. Type into the light green cells and nothing else.
  • Dashboard. Everything calculated for you: total income, total money out, money left over, savings rate, and how your spending splits against the 50/30/20 rule.

The personal budget template for Excel

The Excel version is the full workbook, with dropdown menus for tagging each expense as a Need, a Want, or Save, and conditional formatting that drives the red and green. It opens in Microsoft Excel, Apple Numbers, and LibreOffice, so it works whatever you have on your computer. This is the version most people want if they budget at a desk.

The personal budget template for Google Sheets

If you would rather work in a browser, the Google Sheets version does the same job with nothing to install. Clicking the button above prompts Google to make your own private copy in your Drive. Your copy is yours alone, and editing it changes nothing for anyone else. This is the easiest option if you budget on a phone or share the budget with a partner.

The printable PDF budget template

Some people simply think better on paper, and there is nothing wrong with that. The PDF has the same sections laid out to print cleanly, with space to write in your numbers by hand. It is a good choice for a fridge door or a household binder.

How to actually use it, in one sentence. Open last month's bank statement and fill in the Actual column with what you really spent, not what you think you spent. That single habit is what turns a budget from a wish into a plan.

Questions about the budget template

Is this personal budget template really free?
Yes. There is no payment, no signup, and no email required. Click a download button and the file is yours to keep, edit, and share with anyone who needs it.
Does the personal budget template work in Excel and Google Sheets?
Yes. The Excel workbook opens in Excel, Numbers, and LibreOffice. There is a separate Google Sheets version that makes its own copy in your Drive, so you can use it in a browser with nothing to install. A printable PDF is also included for people who prefer paper.
Do I have to do any of the math myself?
No. Every total, percentage, and color is calculated for you. You type your income and expenses into the light green cells and the workbook handles the rest, including your savings rate and your 50/30/20 split.
What do the red and green colors mean?
Green means you came in under budget or you are hitting your savings target. Red means you spent more than you planned, or a category has run past its target share of your income. The cells change color as you type, so problems are obvious without hunting for them.
What is inside the budget spreadsheet template?
There are three tabs. Start Here explains the layout, Monthly Budget is where you enter income, fixed expenses, variable expenses, savings, and debt payoff, and Dashboard shows your income, money out, what is left, your savings rate, and your 50/30/20 breakdown at a glance.
Can I use this budget template for a monthly budget?
Yes, it is built as a monthly budget. Most people copy the Monthly Budget tab once per month so they keep a record of every month and can see their spending trend over time.

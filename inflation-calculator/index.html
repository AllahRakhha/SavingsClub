How Does an Inflation Calculator Work?

An inflation calculator shows how rising prices reduce the purchasing power of your money over time. If inflation averages 3% per year, $100,000 today will only buy $55,368 worth of goods and services in 20 years. This is why keeping all your savings in a low-interest checking account is effectively losing money.

Why Inflation Matters for Your Financial Plan

Inflation is the silent wealth destroyer. If your savings earn 1% but inflation runs at 3%, you're losing 2% purchasing power every year. This is why high-yield savings accounts earning 4%+ APY are important — they outpace inflation and preserve your purchasing power. Use our Savings Calculator to compare growth at different rates.

Historical US Inflation Rates

The long-term average US inflation rate is approximately 3% per year, though it varies significantly. The Federal Reserve targets 2% annual inflation. Recent years have seen higher-than-average inflation, making it especially important to ensure your savings are growing faster than prices are rising.

Inflation and Retirement Planning

If you need $50,000/year in retirement today, you'll need approximately $90,000/year in 20 years at 3% inflation. Your retirement plan must account for this. Social Security includes cost-of-living adjustments, but they often lag actual inflation. Having investments that historically outpace inflation (like stock index funds) is essential for long-term wealth preservation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good inflation rate?

The Federal Reserve targets 2% annual inflation. This is considered healthy for the economy. Historically, US inflation has averaged about 3% per year.

How do I protect my money from inflation?

Invest in assets that historically outpace inflation: stock index funds (averaging ~10% returns), real estate, I Bonds, and high-yield savings accounts. Keeping large amounts in a 0.01% checking account loses purchasing power every year.

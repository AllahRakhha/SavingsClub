How Does a Loan Payoff Calculator Work?

A loan payoff calculator shows you how long it will take to pay off a loan and how much interest you'll pay. More importantly, it shows how extra payments can dramatically shorten your loan term and save thousands in interest. This works for any type of loan — personal loans, student loans, car loans, or mortgages.

The Power of Extra Payments

On a $25,000 loan at 6.5% interest with $500 monthly payments, adding just $100/month saves over $1,800 in interest and pays off the loan 11 months faster. The extra payment goes entirely toward principal, which reduces the amount that accrues interest each month — creating a snowball effect.

Which Loans to Pay Off First

The mathematically optimal strategy is to pay extra on your highest-interest debt first (avalanche method). For most Americans, that's credit cards (18-28% APR), then personal loans, then student loans, then mortgages. Use our Debt Payoff Calculator to compare the snowball and avalanche methods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I pay off my loan early?

Usually yes, especially for high-interest debt. The exception: if your loan rate is very low (under 4%) and you could earn more investing the extra money instead. Always maintain an emergency fund before accelerating debt payoff.

Does paying extra go to principal?

With most loans, extra payments reduce your principal balance directly. Contact your lender to confirm — some require you to specify that extra payments go to principal rather than future payments.

Understanding Mortgages in California

California has the largest housing market in the United States and some of the most unique financial dynamics anywhere. The state combines the highest home prices in the country with one of the lowest effective property tax rates — thanks to a 1978 constitutional amendment called Proposition 13.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in California averages approximately 6.45%, very close to the national average. California rates often stay competitive because national lenders aggressively pursue California borrowers, especially in the jumbo loan segment (loans above $766,550 for most counties, higher in high-cost areas).

Proposition 13: California's Unique Property Tax

Proposition 13 caps California property tax at 1% of assessed value plus local fees, with annual increases limited to 2% per year. The effective statewide rate is approximately 0.71%. For a median $785,000 California home, that's roughly $5,575 per year, or $465 per month.

The catch: when you sell and buy a new home, the property is reassessed at current market value. Long-term owners often pay low taxes on their existing home but face a huge tax increase if they move. Property tax rates by major county:

Median Home Prices Across California

The statewide median in California is approximately $785,000 — but this hides extreme regional variation. Bay Area cities consistently top $1.5 million, while Central Valley cities offer prices closer to the national median. Prices grew approximately 2-3% in 2025, slower than recent years due to high mortgage rates cooling demand.

California First-Time Homebuyer Programs

CalHFA MyHome Assistance

Deferred-payment second mortgage of up to 3.5% of the home price for down payment and closing costs. No monthly payments — repayment is deferred until you sell or refinance.

Forgivable Equity Builder Loan

Up to 10% of the purchase price as a forgivable loan for low-to-moderate income first-time buyers. The loan is forgiven if you stay in the home for at least 5 years — essentially free money for qualifying buyers.

CalPLUS Conventional Loan

A competitive 30-year fixed-rate mortgage paired with CalHFA's Zero Interest Program (ZIP), offering 3% or 4% closing cost grants. Particularly helpful in high-cost California markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in California?
As of May 2026, California mortgage rates average approximately 6.45% for a 30-year fixed loan, close to the national average. California attracts large national lenders competing for high-value loans.
What is Proposition 13?
Prop 13 (1978) caps California property tax at 1% of assessed value plus local fees, with annual increases limited to 2%. When sold, properties are reassessed at current market value. Effective statewide rate is approximately 0.71%, one of the lowest in the U.S.
Why is California homeowners insurance so expensive?
Wildfire risk has caused dramatic increases. State Farm, Allstate, and several major insurers have stopped writing new policies or restricted coverage. The state-run FAIR Plan acts as insurer of last resort but provides limited coverage. Always verify insurance availability BEFORE making an offer.
How much income do I need to buy in California?
For a median $785,000 California home with 10% down, you typically need $185,000-$210,000 in household income. For San Francisco or Silicon Valley (median $1.4M+), $300,000+. California has one of the lowest homeownership rates in the U.S. (~55%) for this reason.
Is now a good time to buy in California?
Prices have flattened since 2022 due to high mortgage rates. The state still faces severe housing shortage. If you plan to stay 7+ years and can afford the monthly payment, California real estate historically appreciates well long-term. If you might move in under 3 years, renting often makes more sense.

Understanding mortgages in Florida

Florida is the third most populous state and one of the fastest growing real estate markets in America. The combination of no state income tax, warm climate, and a growing job market has driven heavy migration from northern states like New York, New Jersey, and Illinois. But Florida's housing market comes with one major catch: a homeowners insurance crisis that has made owning a home far more expensive than it appears on paper.

The current 30 year fixed mortgage rate in Florida averages approximately 6.49%, slightly below the national average. Florida's median home price of $410,000 is moderate compared to coastal states like California or New York — but the true cost of homeownership in many areas is dominated by hurricane insurance, which can rival or exceed the property tax bill.

The Florida homeowners insurance crisis

Insurance is the single biggest financial surprise for new Florida homebuyers. Statewide average homeowners insurance is $4,500–$6,000 per year, roughly 3x the national average. Coastal properties and homes in hurricane-prone counties often pay $8,000–$15,000 per year. Many of the largest national insurers have left Florida entirely or stopped writing new policies including State Farm, Farmers, AAA, and parts of Progressive's portfolio.

The state run Citizens Property Insurance Corporation acts as insurer of last resort but is being deliberately downsized, and the state is actively pushing existing Citizens policyholders into private market alternatives. New construction often has lower premiums than older homes due to updated building codes (post 2002 construction with hurricane straps and impact windows).

Before making any offer in Florida, get a property specific insurance quote — not a statewide estimate. In some areas, you may discover no standard insurer will write you a policy at all, leaving you to rely on Citizens or risk pools at higher cost. Lenders require insurance to close. If you can't get insurance, you can't close the loan.

Florida property tax & the Homestead Exemption

Florida's effective property tax rate of 0.91% is below the national average. For a median $410,000 home, that's approximately $3,730 per year, or $311 per month before exemptions. Two powerful protections reduce this further for primary residents:

🏠 Florida Homestead Exemption & Save Our Homes
Homestead Exemption — up to $50,000. Reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by up to $50,000 ($25,000 from all property taxes plus another $25,000 from non school taxes). Saves typical Florida homeowners $750–$1,200 per year. You must file by March 1 of your first full year in the home or you lose it for that year.
Save Our Homes Cap — 3% annual limit. Caps annual assessment increases at 3% per year (or the rate of inflation, whichever is lower). Protects long term homeowners from rapidly rising tax bills during real estate booms. Only applies once you have an active Homestead Exemption filed.
Portability. When you sell and buy another Florida home, you can transfer up to $500,000 of your Save Our Homes tax savings benefit to the new property. This is especially valuable for long term owners moving within Florida.

Florida property tax rates vary modestly by county. Coastal urban counties pay slightly more; inland and northern counties pay slightly less. The variation is small compared to states like New York or Texas — but still worth using in your calculation:

Florida first time homebuyer programs

Florida offers some of the most generous first time buyer assistance in the United States, particularly for essential workers. The Hometown Heroes program is one of the largest state level DPA programs in the country.

Florida Hometown Heroes

Up to $35,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance for full time Florida employees including teachers, law enforcement, firefighters, healthcare workers, military, active service members, and certain other essential workers. One of the most generous state programs in America. Income and purchase price limits apply by county.

Florida Assist (Florida Housing)

Provides up to $10,000 in down payment assistance as a 0% interest, deferred payment second mortgage. Repayment is only required when you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage. Stackable with other Florida Housing programs.

HFA Preferred Conventional Loan

A competitive 30 year fixed rate conventional mortgage with reduced mortgage insurance for income qualified first time buyers. Often paired with down payment assistance from one of the programs above to layer multiple forms of help.

Florida Bond Loan Program

Below market interest rates for income qualified first time buyers. Administered through participating lenders statewide. Particularly useful when general market rates are high.

SavingsClub Tips for Florida Buyers
Three Florida specific things most out-of-state buyers don't know
1
Get a property specific insurance quote BEFORE making an offer, not after. Major insurers have left Florida. In coastal counties and some inland areas, you may discover no standard carrier will write you a policy at all leaving you with Citizens Property Insurance at higher cost and reduced coverage. Lenders require insurance to close. No insurance = no closing. This kills deals every week in Florida. Always get a quote on the specific address during your inspection period.
2
File for the Homestead Exemption by March 1 of your first full year — or lose $750–$1,200/year permanently. Most new homeowners miss the filing deadline and don't realize they can't claim retroactively. The Save Our Homes 3% cap on annual assessment increases only kicks in after you have the exemption filed. Missing one year means paying full assessment increases until you fix it, plus losing that year's savings forever.
3
Be careful with Florida condos read the HOA reserve study before signing anything. After the 2021 Surfside collapse, Florida now requires mandatory structural inspections for condos 30+ years old, and many older buildings are hitting owners with $30,000–$100,000+ special assessments. Newer condos and townhouses are also being repriced for insurance. Always request the HOA's most recent reserve study, recent special assessment history, and master insurance policy before the inspection period ends.
Frequently Asked Questions
What is the average mortgage rate in Florida in 2026?
As of June 2026, Florida 30 year fixed mortgage rates average approximately 6.49%, slightly below the national average of 6.53%. Florida has a competitive lending market due to high transaction volume, new construction activity, and continuous out-of-state buyer migration.
Does Florida have a state income tax?
No. Florida is one of seven U.S. states with no state income tax. This makes Florida especially attractive for high earners, retirees collecting investment income, and remote workers. Combined with no state estate tax, Florida is one of the most tax friendly states for retirement though the savings can be partially or fully offset by property insurance costs.
Why is homeowners insurance so expensive in Florida?
Florida faces hurricane risk plus an unusually high frequency of property insurance lawsuits. Many major insurers (State Farm, Farmers, AAA, parts of Progressive) have left or restricted coverage. Statewide average is approximately $4,500–$6,000/year. Coastal homes often pay $8,000 to $15,000 or more. Always get a property specific insurance quote before making an offer and during your inspection period, confirm the policy will actually be issued at the quoted rate.
What is the Florida Homestead Exemption?
Florida's Homestead Exemption reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by up to $50,000. You must file by March 1 of the year following your purchase. Florida also has the Save Our Homes amendment, which caps annual assessment increases at 3% protecting long term homeowners from rapidly rising tax bills during real estate booms. You can also "port" up to $500,000 of accumulated Save Our Homes savings to a new Florida home when you move.
Where in Florida is most affordable?
Northern Florida (Tallahassee, Jacksonville) and inland Central Florida offer the most affordable home prices, generally under $325,000 median. Avoiding coastal counties also dramatically reduces insurance costs often saving $3,000–$6,000 per year. Trade off: fewer high paying jobs and longer drives to beaches. Cape Coral and inland parts of Lee County are popular middle ground options that balance price, access to coast, and somewhat lower insurance.
What should I watch out for with Florida condos?
After the 2021 Surfside collapse, Florida now requires structural inspections for condos 30+ years old, and many older buildings have hit owners with $30,000–$100,000+ special assessments. Always request the HOA's reserve study, recent special assessment history, master insurance policy, and any milestone inspection reports BEFORE the end of your inspection period. New construction and well funded HOAs are generally lower risk than older buildings with underfunded reserves.

Understanding Mortgages in Florida

Florida is the third-most-populous state and one of the fastest-growing real estate markets in America. The combination of no state income tax, warm climate, and growing job market has driven heavy migration from northern states like New York and Illinois. But Florida's housing market comes with one major catch: a homeowners insurance crisis that has made owning a home far more expensive than it appears on paper.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Florida averages approximately 6.49%, slightly below the national average. Florida's median home price of $410,000 is moderate compared to coastal states like California or New York — but the true cost of homeownership is dominated by hurricane insurance, which can rival or exceed the mortgage payment itself.

The Florida Homeowners Insurance Crisis

Florida insurance is the single biggest financial surprise for new homebuyers in the state. Statewide average homeowners insurance is $4,500-$6,000 per year, roughly 3x the national average. Coastal properties and homes in hurricane-prone counties often pay $8,000-$15,000+ per year. Many of the largest national insurers have left Florida entirely.

Before making any offer in Florida, get a property-specific insurance quote — not a statewide estimate. The state-run Citizens Property Insurance Corporation acts as insurer of last resort but is being deliberately downsized. New construction often has lower premiums than older homes due to updated building codes.

Florida Property Tax & Homestead Exemption

Florida's effective property tax rate of 0.91% is below the national average. For a median $410,000 home, that's approximately $3,730 per year, or $311 per month. Two powerful protections reduce this further for primary residents:

Homestead Exemption ($50,000):
Reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by up to $50,000 ($25,000 from all property taxes, plus another $25,000 from non-school taxes). Saves typical Florida homeowners $750-$1,200 per year.
Save Our Homes Cap:
Limits annual assessment increases to 3% per year (or the rate of inflation, whichever is lower). This protects long-term homeowners from skyrocketing tax bills during real estate booms.

Median Home Prices Across Florida

Florida's median home price of $410,000 hides significant regional variation. South Florida and coastal areas command premium prices, while inland and northern Florida remain affordable. Prices grew approximately 4-5% in 2025, slightly faster than the national average due to continued in-migration.

Florida First-Time Homebuyer Programs

Florida Hometown Heroes

Up to $35,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance for full-time Florida employees including teachers, law enforcement, firefighters, healthcare workers, military, and active service members. One of the most generous state programs in the country.

Florida Assist (Florida Housing)

Provides up to $10,000 in down payment assistance as a 0% interest, deferred-payment second mortgage. Repayment is only required when you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage.

HFA Preferred Conventional Loan

A competitive 30-year fixed-rate conventional mortgage with reduced mortgage insurance for income-qualified first-time buyers. Often paired with down payment assistance from one of the programs above.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Florida?
As of May 2026, Florida mortgage rates average approximately 6.49% for a 30-year fixed loan. Florida has a competitive lending market due to high volume of new construction and out-of-state buyers.
Does Florida have state income tax?
No. Florida is one of seven states with no state income tax. This makes Florida especially attractive for high earners, retirees collecting investment income, and remote workers. Combined with no estate tax, Florida is a tax-friendly state for retirement.
Why is homeowners insurance so expensive in Florida?
Florida faces hurricane risk plus an unusual amount of property insurance lawsuits. Many major insurers (Farmers, AAA, Progressive in some areas) have left or restricted coverage. Statewide average is approximately $4,500-$6,000/year. Coastal homes often pay $8,000-$15,000+. Always get a property-specific insurance quote before making an offer.
What is the Florida homestead exemption?
Florida's homestead exemption reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by up to $50,000. You must file by March 1 of your first year in the home. Florida also has the Save Our Homes amendment, which caps annual assessment increases at 3% — protecting long-term homeowners from rapidly rising tax bills.
Where in Florida is most affordable?
Northern Florida (Tallahassee, Jacksonville) and inland Central Florida offer the most affordable home prices, generally under $325,000 median. Avoiding coastal counties also dramatically reduces insurance costs — often saving $3,000-$6,000 per year. Trade-off: fewer high-paying jobs and longer drives to beaches.

Understanding Mortgages in Georgia

Georgia is one of the fastest-growing housing markets in the South. Atlanta metro alone added over 70,000 residents in recent years, and the state's combination of moderate prices, no state estate tax, and strong job growth has attracted heavy migration from California, New York, and Illinois. The cost of living advantage over coastal states is real but Atlanta property taxes and HOA fees can surprise out-of-state buyers.

Georgia's 30-year fixed mortgage rate averages approximately 6.50%, very close to the national average. The state has strong lending competition in metro Atlanta Georgia is one of the top mortgage origination markets in the Southeast, drawing aggressive pricing from national lenders.

Georgia Property Tax: County Variations

Georgia's effective property tax rate of 0.92% is below the national average. For a median $335,000 Georgia home, that's approximately $3,080 per year, or $257 per month. But metro Atlanta counties charge meaningfully higher rates than rural Georgia:

Georgia offers a homestead exemption that reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by $2,000 (statewide minimum), with many counties offering larger exemptions. Seniors 65+ may qualify for school tax exemptions worth thousands per year. Apply with your county tax assessor it's not automatic.

Georgia's New Flat Tax System

In 2024, Georgia transitioned from a graduated income tax (1%-5.75%) to a flat 5.39% rate. The rate is scheduled to drop to 4.99% over time if state revenue targets are met. This simplifies tax planning and modestly reduces taxes for middle-income earners. Atlanta does NOT impose a city income tax unlike Philadelphia or New York City.

Georgia is generally retiree-friendly: it does not tax Social Security benefits, and offers a retirement income exclusion of up to $65,000 per person ($130,000 for couples) for taxpayers age 65+. This makes Georgia popular for retirees moving from higher-tax states like New Jersey or Illinois.

Georgia First-Time Homebuyer Programs

Georgia Dream Homeownership Program

Up to $10,000 in down payment assistance for first-time buyers ($12,500 for "Protectors, Educators, and Nurses" including police, firefighters, military, teachers, and healthcare workers). 0% interest, deferred-payment second mortgage.

Georgia Dream PEN Program

Specifically for Protectors, Educators, and Nurses. Combines down payment assistance with competitive 30-year fixed mortgage rates. Income limits apply but are generous compared to most state programs.

Invest Atlanta Down Payment Programs

City of Atlanta offers additional down payment assistance up to $25,000 for income-qualified buyers purchasing within city limits. Can be stacked with Georgia Dream for substantial total assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Georgia?
As of May 2026, Georgia mortgage rates average approximately 6.50% for a 30-year fixed loan, close to the national average. Georgia has strong lending competition, especially in Atlanta metro, which keeps rates competitive.
Is Atlanta property tax high?
Metro Atlanta counties charge 1.0-1.3% effective property tax rates, which is above the Georgia state average of 0.92% but still below the national average of 1.02%. Outside metro Atlanta, rural Georgia counties often charge under 0.80%. Atlanta's combination of moderate prices and reasonable taxes makes it attractive compared to coastal high-tax markets.
Is Georgia good for retirees?
Yes — Georgia is one of the better Southern states for retirees. It does not tax Social Security benefits and offers a retirement income exclusion of up to $65,000 per person ($130,000 for couples) for taxpayers 65+. Combined with the moderate cost of living and warm climate, Georgia has become a popular retirement destination, especially in mountain areas (Blue Ridge) and coastal cities (Savannah).
Should I buy in Atlanta or a smaller GA city?
Atlanta offers strong job market (Coca-Cola, Delta, Home Depot, UPS, CDC, plus growing tech sector) and major airport access but median prices ($395K) are above the state average. Smaller cities like Athens, Augusta, and Macon offer median prices under $315K with reasonable job markets. Savannah offers coastal lifestyle at $305K median. Match your decision to your job and lifestyle priorities.
What about HOA fees in Georgia?
Atlanta suburbs frequently have HOAs charging $400-$1,200 per year for typical single family neighborhoods. New construction communities often charge more. Always factor HOA fees into your total housing cost they're not included in standard mortgage calculator estimates. Older homes in established intown Atlanta neighborhoods are often HOA-free.

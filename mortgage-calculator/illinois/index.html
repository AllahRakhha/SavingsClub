Understanding Mortgages in Illinois

Illinois offers affordable home prices but has the 2nd highest effective property tax rate in America (2.08%) meaning property tax often becomes the largest single line item in your monthly housing cost, sometimes exceeding the principal-and-interest portion of your mortgage payment. Understanding this tradeoff is critical before buying in Illinois.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Illinois averages approximately 6.50%, almost identical to the national average. Chicago has a competitive lending market with strong national and regional lenders. Downstate Illinois often sees lower rates from local credit unions.

Illinois Property Tax: The Real Cost

Illinois ranks 2nd in the nation for effective property tax (only New Jersey is higher). The statewide effective rate of 2.08% means a median $275,000 IL home costs approximately $5,720/year, or $477/month, in property taxes alone.

High Illinois property taxes are driven by unfunded public pension obligations of school districts, municipalities, and county governments. This is structural, not seasonal Illinois property taxes have grown faster than inflation for 20+ years and are expected to continue rising. Effective rates by major county:

Illinois First-Time Homebuyer Programs

IHDA Smart Buy

Up to $40,000 in down payment assistance plus student loan payoff for first-time buyers (or buyers in target areas). The state pays off up to 15% of your student loan balance (capped) at closing. One of the most generous programs in the country.

IHDA Access Forgivable

Up to $6,000 in forgivable down payment and closing cost assistance. The loan is forgiven over 10 years if you stay in the home. Combines with IHDA 30-year fixed-rate mortgage.

Opening Doors Down Payment Assistance

$6,000 in forgivable down payment and closing cost help for low-income, first-time buyers. Combines with IHDA loan programs and can be paired with other federal/local assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Illinois?
As of May 2026, Illinois mortgage rates average approximately 6.50% for a 30-year fixed loan. Chicago's competitive lending market with major banks like Chase, US Bank, and BMO Harris keeps rates close to the national average.
Why are Illinois property taxes so high?
Illinois has massive unfunded pension liabilities for state and local government workers (teachers, police, fire). Property taxes fund these obligations along with schools and local services. Rates have grown faster than inflation for 20+ years. Lake, Will, McHenry, and Kane counties have rates above 2.4% among the highest county rates in America.
Should I buy in Chicago or the suburbs?
Chicago city has lower property tax rates (1.85%) than nearly all suburbs (2.1-2.65%) but higher home prices and worse schools in many areas. North Shore suburbs (Evanston, Glenview, Wilmette) have excellent schools but property tax often $15,000-$25,000/year on a $700K home. Naperville and Hinsdale offer the schools/safety balance most families seek but at premium prices.
Are Illinois homes still a good investment?
Mixed. Illinois has slow population growth (some years negative) which limits long-term appreciation. Home values grow approximately 2-3% per year well below national average of 4-5%. However, low entry prices ($275K median) mean Illinois homes can still be a reasonable buy for owner-occupants, especially if you avoid Chicago's most expensive suburbs.
What's the Illinois homestead exemption?
Illinois offers a General Homestead Exemption that reduces taxable equalized assessed value by $6,000-$10,000 (depending on county) for primary residences. Cook County adds extra exemptions for seniors ($8,000), disabled persons ($2,000), and disabled veterans (varies). Apply through your county assessor.

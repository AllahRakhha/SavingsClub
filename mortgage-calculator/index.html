Looking for a Mortgage Lender?

SavingsClub doesn't currently partner with mortgage lenders we'd rather give you honest education than push you toward whoever pays us the most. Here's how to actually find the right mortgage for your situation.

The shopping rule that saves the most money: Get pre-approval quotes from at least 3 lenders within a 14-day window. Multiple mortgage credit pulls within 14 days count as ONE inquiry on your credit score (per FICO and VantageScore rules) — so there's no penalty for shopping aggressively. A 0.25% rate difference on a $350,000 loan is $18,000 over 30 years. Don't take the first quote.

✓ Where to start your search

  • Your existing bank or credit union — often best rates for current customers; relationship discounts of 0.10–0.25% common
  • Local credit unions typically 0.10–0.25% below national lenders for well qualified buyers
  • Online lenders Rocket Mortgage, Better.com, Zillow Home Loans; fast pre-approvals, often competitive rates
  • HUD-approved housing counselors FREE, unbiased mortgage advice; find one at hud.gov/findcounselor
  • Mortgage brokers work with multiple lenders on your behalf; useful if you have unique circumstances

⚠ Watch out for these traps

  • Rate quotes that aren't APR always compare APR (which includes fees), not the "interest rate" alone
  • Discount points pitched as savings paying points only makes sense if you'll hold the loan 5+ years; run the breakeven math
  • "Lender credits" they raise your rate to reduce closing costs; useful for short holds, costly for long holds
  • Aggressive prequalification timelines pressure to lock TODAY usually means the rate isn't actually competitive
  • Higher than quoted closing costs at table your Loan Estimate (3 days after applying) locks in most fees; compare the Closing Disclosure carefully

Understanding Mortgage Payments

Your monthly mortgage payment includes more than just principal and interest. Property taxes, homeowners insurance, PMI (if your down payment is less than 20%), and HOA fees all add to the total. This calculator gives you the complete picture so there are no surprises.

PMI Explained

Private Mortgage Insurance is required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. It typically costs 0.5-1% of the loan amount annually. On a $280,000 loan, that's $1,400-$2,800 per year added to your payment. PMI is removed once you reach 20% equity.

HOA Fees

If your home is in a homeowners association (common in condos, townhomes, and planned communities), monthly HOA fees can add $100-$700+ to your housing cost. These fees pay for shared amenities, maintenance, and reserves.

Amortization Explained

Amortization is the process of paying off a loan with regular payments. In the early years of a mortgage, most of your payment goes to interest. As the balance decreases, more goes to principal. By the final years, you're paying mostly principal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much house can I afford?

A common guideline is that your total housing payment (including taxes and insurance) should be no more than 28% of your gross monthly income. On a $6,000/month income, that's $1,680 for housing.

Understanding Mortgages in New York

New York has one of the most complex real estate markets in America. The state combines extreme regional variation (Manhattan median exceeds $1.15M while Buffalo is under $200K), unique buyer-paid taxes that don't exist in other states, and high state income tax. New York real estate transactions can include closing costs of 4-6% of purchase price significantly higher than the 2-3% typical elsewhere.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in New York averages approximately 6.51%, very close to the national average. Co-ops (a unique NYC ownership structure) often require higher down payments (20-30%) and have specific lender requirements. Most national lenders compete heavily in NY but the unique market structure adds complexity.

NYC Mansion Tax & Mortgage Recording Tax

New York City buyers face two extra taxes that surprise out-of-state buyers:

NYC Mansion Tax (buyer pays):
Additional tax on residential purchases of $1M or more. 1% on $1M-$1.99M, scaling up to 3.9% on purchases over $25M. A $1.5M Brooklyn townhouse triggers $15,000 in mansion tax.
Mortgage Recording Tax (NYC):
1.8% on loans under $500K, 2.8% on loans $500K+ within NYC. A $700K mortgage triggers approximately $19,600 in recording tax paid at closing.
Transfer Tax:
NY State 0.4% transfer tax. NYC adds 1% on properties up to $500K, 1.425% above. Usually paid by the seller but sometimes negotiable.

New York Property Tax: Wildly Variable

New York's statewide property tax average of 1.40% tells you almost nothing — actual rates vary from under 0.85% (NYC, due to assessment caps) to over 3% (some Long Island and Westchester suburbs). For a median $475,000 home statewide, you'd pay approximately $6,650/year but suburban NYC owners commonly pay $15,000 $25,000/year on similar sized homes.

New York First-Time Homebuyer Programs

SONYMA (State of New York Mortgage Agency)

Competitive 30-year fixed-rate mortgages with down payment assistance up to $15,000 or 3% of the home price. Income and purchase price limits apply by county. SONYMA loans are originated through participating lenders statewide.

NYC HomeFirst Down Payment Assistance

Up to $100,000 in forgivable down payment and closing cost assistance for low-to-moderate income first-time buyers purchasing in NYC. The loan is forgiven over 10-15 years if you stay in the home.

First-Time Homebuyer Credit (NYC)

NYC offers a partial Mortgage Recording Tax credit for first-time buyers on loans under $200,000 — saves approximately $3,000-$5,000 at closing. Income limits and other eligibility requirements apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in New York?
As of May 2026, New York mortgage rates average approximately 6.51% for a 30-year fixed loan, close to the national average. Co-op loans (NYC specifically) may have different rates and require board approval in addition to lender approval.
What is the NYC mansion tax?
The NYC mansion tax is an additional buyer-paid tax on residential purchases of $1M or more. It scales from 1% (at $1M) up to 3.9% (over $25M). On a $1.5M Brooklyn home, you pay $15,000 mansion tax at closing on top of other taxes. The tax applies to purchase price, not just to the amount over $1M.
Why does New York property tax vary so much?
Local school districts and municipalities set their own rates. Wealthy suburbs with excellent schools (Westchester, Long Island) levy 2-3% effective rates. NYC pays a lower 0.85% but with severe assessment caps that distort values. Western New York pays the highest rates (2.5-3%) but on much cheaper homes.
Should I buy upstate or downstate?
Upstate (Buffalo, Rochester, Albany, Syracuse) offers some of the most affordable housing in America — median prices under $250K. Property tax rates are higher (2.5-3%) but on cheaper homes. Downstate offers strong job markets and appreciation potential but at much higher entry prices. Match your decision to job location and lifestyle.
What closing costs should I expect in New York?
NY closing costs are among the highest in the U.S. — typically 4-6% of purchase price for buyers (vs 2-3% national average). This includes Mortgage Recording Tax, mansion tax (if applicable), attorney fees ($1,500-$3,000 — NY is one of the few states that requires attorneys at closing), title insurance, transfer taxes, and various smaller fees. Budget more for closing in NY than other states.

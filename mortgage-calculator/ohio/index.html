Understanding Mortgages in Ohio

Ohio is the most affordable of America's top 10 most populous states for homebuyers, with a median home price of approximately $235,000 about 44% below the national median. The state combines low entry prices with moderate property tax (1.36%) and a graduated income tax that maxes out at 3.5%. Ohio has seen modest economic growth and continued in-migration from higher-cost states, particularly in the Columbus and Cincinnati metros.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Ohio averages approximately 6.47%, slightly below the national average. Ohio has a strong network of community banks and credit unions that often offer rates 0.10-0.20% below national lenders for well-qualified buyers, especially in smaller cities.

Ohio offers a Homestead Exemption that reduces taxable value by $25,000 for homeowners over age 65 or permanently disabled. The Owner-Occupancy Credit gives a 2.5% reduction in voted property taxes on primary residences. Apply through your county auditor.

Ohio's Layered Income Tax

Ohio's state income tax is graduated, reaching a top rate of 3.5% on income above $115,000. But Ohio is unusual because most cities charge their own income tax on top of state tax:

Columbus: 2.5% city income tax
Cleveland: 2.5% city income tax
Cincinnati: 1.8% city income tax
Toledo: 2.5% city income tax
Akron: 2.5% city income tax
Dayton: 2.5% city income tax

A Columbus homeowner earning $100K pays approximately 3.0% state income tax + 2.5% city income tax = 5.5% total. Always factor city income tax when comparing Ohio cities to other states. Suburbs of major Ohio cities often have lower or no city tax.

Ohio homeowners insurance is below national average typically $1,300-$1,700 per year. The state has minimal natural disaster risk (no hurricanes, low earthquake risk). Tornadoes are the main weather concern in western Ohio but rarely affect insurance significantly.

Ohio First-Time Homebuyer Programs

OHFA YourChoice! Down Payment Assistance

Choice of 2.5% or 5% of the home's purchase price toward down payment and closing costs. Provided as a 0% interest, forgivable second mortgage with no monthly payment. Combines with OHFA 30-year fixed-rate mortgage.

Grants for Grads

Recent college graduates (within 48 months) get 2.5% or 5% down payment assistance plus a discounted mortgage rate. Available to anyone with an associate, bachelor, master, or professional degree.

Ohio Heroes

Discounted mortgage rates (typically 0.25% below market) for full-time teachers, firefighters, EMTs, peace officers, healthcare workers, and active military service members. Can combine with down payment assistance programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Ohio?
As of May 2026, Ohio mortgage rates average approximately 6.47% for a 30-year fixed loan. Local Ohio credit unions (Telhio, Wright-Patt, KEMBA) often offer rates 0.10-0.20% below national lenders for well-qualified buyers.
Why is Cleveland so much cheaper than Columbus?
Cleveland and Columbus have very different economic trajectories. Columbus has strong tech, healthcare, and university job growth (Intel investing $20B+ in semiconductor manufacturing); home values have grown 8-12% per year. Cleveland has had decades of population loss, slower job growth, and aging housing stock keeping median prices around $135K. Cleveland offers extraordinary value for cash buyers, but appreciation is slower.
Does Ohio have city income tax?
Yes — most Ohio cities charge their own income tax of 1.0-2.5% on top of state income tax. Columbus, Cleveland, Toledo, Akron, and Dayton charge 2.5%; Cincinnati charges 1.8%. You pay this even if you live outside the city but work there (RITA tax). Ohio is one of the only states with such widespread city income taxes factor this into total tax burden calculations.
Is Ohio a good place to buy investment property?
Ohio has been a popular state for out-of-state real estate investors due to low entry prices and strong rent-to-price ratios. Cleveland and Toledo offer some of the best cash flow numbers in America. Risks include slow appreciation in struggling neighborhoods, higher tenant turnover, and significant ongoing maintenance on older housing stock. Strong markets like Columbus and Cincinnati suburbs offer better balance of cash flow and appreciation.
Why is Columbus growing so fast?
Columbus is one of the fastest-growing US metros due to Intel's $20B+ semiconductor manufacturing investment, expansion at Ohio State University, strong healthcare/insurance employers (Nationwide, OhioHealth, JPMorgan Chase), and tech company relocations. Population is projected to add 700K+ residents through 2050. Home values have grown 8-12% annually currently the best Ohio market for long-term appreciation.

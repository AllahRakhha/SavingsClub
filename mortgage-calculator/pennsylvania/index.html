Understanding Mortgages in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania is one of the most affordable Northeast states for homebuyers, with a median home price of approximately $265,000 about 37% below the national median. The state combines a low flat state income tax (3.07%) with above-average property tax (1.43% effective), creating a tradeoff that benefits high earners but hurts homeowners with valuable property.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Pennsylvania averages approximately 6.48%, slightly below the national average. PA has a competitive lending market with many regional credit unions and community banks offering rates 0.10-0.25% below national lenders for well-qualified buyers.

Pennsylvania's Unique Tax Treatment

Pennsylvania is the only US state with this combination: 3.07% flat state income tax (no brackets, no deductions for most things) BUT no state tax on retirement income. Key implications:

No deduction for 401(k) contributions:
Unlike most states, you pay PA's 3.07% on money going INTO your 401(k). On a $20,000 contribution, that's $614 extra PA tax compared to other states.
No PA tax on 401(k) or IRA withdrawals:
After age 59½, withdrawals are PA-tax-free. This makes PA attractive for retirees.
Social Security and pensions:
Not taxed by PA. One of the most retiree-friendly states.

Pennsylvania First-Time Homebuyer Programs

PHFA Keystone Home Loan

Competitive 30-year fixed rate mortgage through the Pennsylvania Housing Finance Agency. Available to first-time buyers and certain repeat buyers in targeted areas. Income and purchase price limits apply by county.

K-FIT (Keystone Forgivable in Ten Years)

Up to $25,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance, structured as a forgivable second mortgage. Forgiven 10% per year over 10 years if you remain in the home.

Keystone Advantage Assistance Loan

Up to 4% of the purchase price (max $6,000) as a deferred-payment second mortgage at 0% interest. Repayment only required when you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Pennsylvania?
As of May 2026, PA mortgage rates average approximately 6.48% for a 30-year fixed loan. Local credit unions like PSECU, First Commonwealth, and PNC often offer competitive rates 0.10-0.25% below national lenders for well-qualified buyers.
Is Pittsburgh or Philadelphia better for buyers?
Pittsburgh has slightly lower median home prices ($235K vs Philadelphia's $245K) but higher property tax (1.95% vs 0.95%). Pittsburgh offers strong tech/healthcare jobs; Philadelphia has finance and healthcare. Property tax difference can make Philadelphia significantly cheaper to own long-term despite slightly higher purchase price.
How does PA's 3.07% flat tax affect homeownership?
PA's flat tax means homebuyers cannot deduct mortgage interest on state taxes (only federal). However, the flat tax is low — only 3.07%. Combined with no PA tax on retirement income, PA is one of the most tax-friendly states for long-term homeowners planning to retire in place.
What is the PA homestead exemption?
PA's Homestead/Farmstead Exclusion reduces the taxable assessment of your primary residence for school district taxes. Savings vary by district typically $200-$700 per year. Apply through your county assessment office; not automatic.
Are PA closing costs higher than other states?
PA closing costs are moderate typically 2-4% of purchase price for buyers. PA charges 1% Realty Transfer Tax (often split between buyer and seller usually 0.5% each), plus standard mortgage recording fees, title insurance, and inspection costs. Total closing costs on a $265K home are typically $5,000-$10,000.

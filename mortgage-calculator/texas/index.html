Understanding Mortgages in North Carolina

North Carolina is one of the fastest-growing housing markets in the Southeast. Charlotte and Raleigh-Durham have become major tech and banking hubs, drawing tens of thousands of new residents annually from New York, California, and New Jersey. The combination of moderate prices, low property tax, and a declining flat state income tax has made NC a top destination state for both young professionals and retirees.

NC's 30-year fixed mortgage rate averages approximately 6.49%, slightly below the national average. Charlotte is a major banking center (home to Bank of America, Wells Fargo East Coast operations, Truist), which creates intense lender competition and competitive pricing.

Why NC Property Tax Is Low

North Carolina's effective property tax rate of 0.82% is well below the national average. For a median $345,000 NC home, that's approximately $2,830 per year, or $235 per month. The state's relatively low rate is possible because NC has a moderate income tax (4.25%) plus a 4.75% state sales tax the revenue mix spreads costs across multiple sources rather than relying heavily on property taxes.

NC offers homestead exemptions for seniors 65+ ($25,000 reduction in assessed value) and disabled residents. The state also has the Circuit Breaker Tax Deferment Program for seniors and disabled homeowners who meet income limits caps property tax at 4-5% of income.

NC's Declining Flat Tax

NC has a flat state income tax of 4.25% for 2026, scheduled to drop to 3.99% in 2027 if revenue targets are met. This is one of the more attractive flat tax structures in America lower than Pennsylvania's 3.07% only on paper because NC has no city wage taxes. North Carolina also taxes retirement income but offers significant deductions, including a $35,000 deduction for federal retiree spouses and Bailey Settlement protections for state retirees.

NC First-Time Homebuyer Programs

NC Home Advantage Mortgage

30-year fixed-rate mortgage with up to 3% down payment assistance as a 0% interest forgivable second mortgage. The DPA is forgiven over 15 years (1/180 forgiven each month). Available to both first time and repeat buyers.

1st Home Advantage Down Payment

Up to $15,000 in down payment assistance for first time buyers. 0% interest, deferred payment second mortgage forgiven over 15 years. Must be combined with NC Home Advantage Mortgage.

NC Home Advantage Tax Credit (MCC)

A Mortgage Credit Certificate that provides a federal tax credit of up to 30% of annual mortgage interest paid (max $2,000 per year). Available only to first time buyers and only when not using the NC Home Advantage DPA simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in North Carolina?
As of May 2026, NC mortgage rates average approximately 6.49% for a 30-year fixed loan, slightly below the national average. Charlotte's status as a major banking center creates strong lender competition.
Should I buy in Charlotte or Raleigh?
Both are strong markets. Charlotte ($420K median) has banking and finance jobs, an international airport, NBA/NFL teams, and slightly lower property tax (0.85%). Raleigh Durham ($440K) has tech and pharma (Research Triangle Park), three major universities, and the highest rated public schools in the state. Both have appreciated 5 to 7% annually in recent years. Pick based on industry finance/banking favors Charlotte, tech/biotech/research favors Raleigh.
Is Asheville expensive compared to other NC cities?
Yes, Asheville is the most expensive city in North Carolina ($485K median) despite having a smaller economy than Charlotte or Raleigh. This reflects high demand from out of state retirees, remote workers, and second home buyers attracted to the mountain setting and cultural scene. Property tax there is the lowest in the state (Buncombe County 0.65%) Asheville's price problem is supply, not taxes.
What's the situation with NC's coastal real estate?
NC's coast (Outer Banks, Wilmington, Wrightsville Beach, Carolina Beach) faces hurricane risk and rising flood insurance premiums. Always check FEMA flood maps before buying a property in Zone AE or VE will require flood insurance ($1,500 to $8,000/year depending on elevation). Coastal counties also have higher homeowners insurance and may require separate wind/hail policies. The financial calculation should always include these costs upfront.
Is NC good for retirees?
Yes NC has become a top retirement destination. The state doesn't tax Social Security benefits, has reasonable property taxes, mild climate, strong healthcare (Duke, UNC, Wake Forest), and growing senior communities. The Bailey Settlement protects state retirees' pensions from state income tax. Asheville, Wilmington, Pinehurst, and the Outer Banks are popular retirement areas, each with different cost profiles.

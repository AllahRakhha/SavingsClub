What is the average mortgage payment in Texas per month?

The loan payment is the part people quote. It is not the part that surprises them.

On the Texas median home price of about 341,800 dollars, with 10 percent down at a 6.35 percent rate over 30 years, principal and interest work out to roughly 1,914 dollars a month. That is the number a lender leads with. Then reality arrives: property tax, homeowners insurance, and mortgage insurance if you put less than 20 percent down.

Realistic total monthly payment$2,587

So the honest answer to the average Texas monthly mortgage payment is closer to 2,587 dollars than to 1,914 dollars. The gap, about 673 dollars a month, is what people mean when they say Texas homes are cheap but Texas ownership is not.

The 2026 homestead exemption changed everything

This is the single most valuable thing on this page, and most Texas mortgage calculators have not caught up with it.

Texas voters approved Proposition 13 in November 2025, and for the 2026 tax year the mandatory school district homestead exemption rose from 100,000 dollars to 140,000 dollars. Separately, the additional exemption for homeowners aged 65 or older, and for disabled homeowners, jumped from 10,000 dollars to 60,000 dollars, giving seniors a combined 200,000 dollars.

On our median 341,800 dollar home, the standard homestead exemption cuts the property tax from about 456 dollars a month to about 269 dollars a month. That is a saving of roughly 187 dollars every month, or 2,240 dollars a year, for filing one form. A senior homeowner on the same house saves closer to 267 dollars a month.

The exemption is not automatic. You have to apply. File Form 50-114 with your county appraisal district, and it is free. There are companies that will charge you to do it. Do not pay them. You can file up to two years late and still receive back credits, so if you bought a home in Texas and never filed, do it this week. Details are on the Texas Comptroller site.

Why Texas property tax is so high

Texas has no state income tax. That money has to come from somewhere, and it comes from your house. Property tax funds the schools, the roads, and the local services that an income tax would pay for elsewhere, which is why the statewide effective rate runs around 1.5 to 1.6 percent against a national average closer to 1 percent.

The statewide number also hides how much your county matters. Suburban counties around Houston, Dallas, and Austin frequently run from 1.8 to 2.5 percent. Here is the same 341,800 dollar home, with the homestead exemption applied, across the major metros.

Texas mortgage assistance programs

Texas runs some of the more generous down payment assistance in the country, and a surprising number of buyers never hear about it because their lender is not approved to offer it. If you are buying in Texas, ask about these before you sign anything.

My First Texas Home (TDHCA)

The main state program for first-time buyers and veterans. It pairs a 30-year fixed mortgage with up to 5 percent of the loan amount as down payment assistance, structured as a zero interest second lien. On a 300,000 dollar loan that is about 15,000 dollars. The assistance is typically forgiven after three years of living in the home, and repaid if you sell or refinance sooner.

My Choice Texas Home (TDHCA)

The same idea, but you do not have to be a first-time buyer. Assistance generally runs 2 to 5 percent. Some versions carry recapture provisions, meaning a portion may be clawed back if your income rises sharply and you sell within the first several years.

Texas Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)

Genuinely underused. An MCC converts a slice of your annual mortgage interest into a federal tax credit, not a deduction. A credit reduces your tax bill dollar for dollar, and it is capped at 2,000 dollars a year, every year you hold the loan. It can be stacked with down payment assistance, and over a full 30-year loan it is worth real money.

TSAHC: Home Sweet Texas and Homes for Texas Heroes

Run by the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation rather than TDHCA. Homes for Texas Heroes is aimed at teachers, police, firefighters, EMS, corrections officers, and veterans. Both offer down payment assistance and can include an MCC.

City programs, which are often the biggest

Several Texas cities run their own assistance on top of the state programs, and the amounts can be far larger. Houston, Dallas, Austin, San Antonio, and Fort Worth all operate homebuyer assistance programs with their own income limits and residency requirements. If you are buying inside a major city, check the city program before you assume the state program is your ceiling.

How to qualify for Texas down payment assistance

The common requirements across most of these programs:

  • A credit score around 620 or better
  • Household income under your county limit, commonly near 97,000 dollars for a one or two person household, higher in expensive counties
  • A purchase price under your county cap, generally between 350,000 and 500,000 dollars
  • Completion of a HUD-approved homebuyer education course, usually 6 to 8 hours, around 75 dollars
  • Applying through a participating lender. You cannot apply to the agency directly, and many lenders are not approved
The mistake that costs people their assistance. Do not open new credit in the weeks before closing. Financing a car or a sofa can push your debt-to-income ratio above the program limit and get the file rejected after your assistance was already reserved. Wait until after you close. How to choose a mortgage lender in Dallas, or anywhere in Texas

We do not publish a ranked list of "best lenders," because a list like that goes stale within weeks and usually reflects who is paying for placement rather than who will actually quote you the best rate. Here is what genuinely works instead.

Get three Loan Estimates on the same day. Mortgage rates move daily. A quote from Monday and a quote from Thursday are not comparable, and lenders know it. Ask three lenders for a written Loan Estimate on the same day and put them side by side.

Compare the APR, not the rate. The headline rate is marketing. The annual percentage rate includes the fees, and the gap between two lenders is routinely half a percentage point, which on a 400,000 dollar loan is worth tens of thousands of dollars over the life of the loan.

Ask first whether they are approved for TDHCA or TSAHC. If you want down payment assistance, this question eliminates most lenders immediately and saves you weeks. Ask it on the first call, not the fifth.

Rate shopping does not wreck your credit. Multiple mortgage inquiries inside a short window count as a single inquiry. The fear of "too many credit checks" costs Texas buyers far more than the inquiries ever would.

The insurance problem nobody warns you about

Texas has some of the most expensive homeowners insurance in the country, and it is not mainly about hurricanes. It is hail. North Texas in particular takes enormous hail losses, and premiums reflect it statewide.

On the coast it gets more complicated. You may need separate windstorm coverage through the Texas Windstorm Insurance Association, and flood insurance is always a separate policy through the National Flood Insurance Program, never part of a standard homeowners policy. Check the FEMA flood map for any property before you make an offer.

Get an insurance quote on a specific address before your offer, not after. Buyers routinely budget 150 dollars a month for insurance and discover the real number is 350 dollars, which can change what they can afford entirely.

Before you buy in Texas

Three numbers matter more than the listing price.

Your take-home pay. Texas has no state income tax, so your paycheck goes further here than almost anywhere. Run it through our Texas paycheck calculator and use that number, not your gross salary, when you decide what you can afford.

Your emergency fund. A house generates emergencies. A new roof after a hailstorm is a Texas rite of passage. Size your cushion with the emergency fund calculator before you drain your savings into a down payment.

Your full monthly budget. The calculator at the top of this page gives you the true payment. Put it into the budget planner and see whether it actually fits, because a mortgage you can technically qualify for and a mortgage you can comfortably live with are not the same thing.

Texas mortgage questions people ask

What is the average mortgage payment in Texas per month?
On the Texas median home price of about 341,800 dollars with 10 percent down at a 6.35 percent rate, principal and interest come to roughly 1,914 dollars a month. Add property tax, insurance, and PMI and the realistic all-in payment is closer to 2,587 dollars a month once the homestead exemption is applied. Property tax and insurance are what push the Texas number well above the loan payment alone.
How much is the Texas homestead exemption in 2026?
The mandatory school district homestead exemption rose to 140,000 dollars for the 2026 tax year, up from 100,000 dollars, after voters approved Proposition 13 in November 2025. Homeowners aged 65 or older, and disabled homeowners, get an additional 60,000 dollars, up from 10,000 dollars, for a combined 200,000 dollars. On a median Texas home this is worth roughly 187 dollars a month.
Why is Texas property tax so high?
Because Texas has no state income tax, property tax carries the load for schools, roads, and local services. The statewide effective rate runs around 1.5 to 1.6 percent, well above the national average, and suburban counties around Houston, Dallas, and Austin often run from 1.8 to 2.5 percent. You keep more of your paycheck in Texas and pay more on your house.
What mortgage assistance is available in Texas?
The Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs runs My First Texas Home for first-time buyers and My Choice Texas Home for repeat buyers, both offering up to 5 percent of the loan amount as down payment assistance. The Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation runs Home Sweet Texas and Homes for Texas Heroes for teachers, first responders, and veterans. The Texas Mortgage Credit Certificate converts part of your annual mortgage interest into a federal tax credit worth up to 2,000 dollars a year.
How do I qualify for Texas down payment assistance?
Typical requirements are a credit score around 620, income under your county limit, a purchase price under your county cap, and completion of a HUD-approved homebuyer education course. You must apply through a participating lender rather than directly to the agency. Income limits in most counties run near 97,000 dollars for a one or two person household, and purchase price caps generally fall between 350,000 and 500,000 dollars depending on the county.
Do I have to pay back Texas down payment assistance?
Usually not, if you stay in the home. The assistance is typically structured as a zero interest second lien that is forgiven after a set period, commonly three years for My First Texas Home. If you sell or refinance before that period ends, the assistance generally has to be repaid. Some programs also include recapture provisions if your income rises substantially and you sell within the first several years.
How do I choose a mortgage lender in Dallas?
Get written Loan Estimates from at least three lenders on the same day, because rates move daily and only a same-day comparison is fair. Compare the annual percentage rate rather than the headline rate, since the APR includes fees. If you want down payment assistance, confirm first that the lender is approved for TDHCA or TSAHC programs, because many are not. Rate gaps of half a percentage point between lenders are common, and on a 400,000 dollar loan that is worth tens of thousands of dollars over the life of the loan.
Is Texas homeowners insurance expensive?
Yes. Texas has some of the highest homeowners insurance premiums in the country because of hail, wind, and hurricane exposure. Hail alone drives large claims across North Texas. Coastal counties may also require separate windstorm coverage through the Texas Windstorm Insurance Association, and flood insurance is a separate policy from the National Flood Insurance Program. Always get an insurance quote before you make an offer, not after.

Understanding mortgages in Virginia

Virginia is a state of dramatic regional contrasts when it comes to housing. The statewide median home price of approximately $385,000 hides extreme variation — Northern Virginia (Arlington, Alexandria, Fairfax, Loudoun) commands $650,000–$800,000+ medians thanks to federal government jobs, defense contractors, Amazon HQ2, and the Dulles tech corridor. Meanwhile, Richmond ($325K), Virginia Beach ($385K), Norfolk ($265K), and Roanoke ($245K) remain affordable compared to most East Coast cities.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Virginia averages approximately 6.50%, near the national average. Virginia has the second-highest military and federal employment concentration in America (behind California), which makes VA loans and federal credit unions (Navy Federal, Pentagon Federal, Apple Federal) hugely important — they often offer rates 0.15–0.25% below national lenders.

Why Northern Virginia (NOVA) is so much more expensive

Northern Virginia is part of the Washington DC metro area, with home prices that look more like Bay Area or Boston than the rest of Virginia. Arlington County medians have crossed $790,000 following Amazon HQ2's arrival, while Alexandria sits at $650K, Fairfax County at $720K, and Loudoun County at $695K. The region has unusually high household incomes ($120K–$180K median in many ZIP codes) driven by federal government employees, defense contractors (Lockheed, Booz Allen, SAIC), and tech companies (Amazon, AWS, Microsoft Dulles campus).

Virginia has the second-highest concentration of military and veterans in America (behind California), so VA loans (0% down + no PMI) are the single most powerful homebuying tool here. Norfolk hosts the world's largest naval base plus the Pentagon, Quantico, Langley AFB, and Fort Belvoir. Lenders like Navy Federal, Pentagon Federal, USAA, and Veterans United specialize in VA loans and typically close in 30–45 days. The funding fee (2.15 to 3.3%) is waived for veterans with any service-connected disability rating.

Virginia property tax by county

Virginia's effective property tax rate of 0.82% is well below the national average one of the best in the Mid-Atlantic region. For a median $385,000 Virginia home, that's approximately $3,157 per year, or $263 per month. But variation between localities is significant — Norfolk's effective rate (1.30%) is nearly 60% higher than Henrico County's (0.87%).

Virginia's disabled veteran exemption

Virginia provides a FULL property tax exemption on the primary residence of veterans rated 100% permanently and totally disabled by the VA (or surviving unremarried spouses of veterans who died from service-connected causes). NO income limit. Saves the entire annual property tax bill typically $4,000 to $8,000 per year in Northern Virginia. Apply through your locality's commissioner of revenue with your VA disability rating documentation. Virginia also offers an Elderly and Disabled Real Estate Tax Relief program (age 65+ or permanently disabled) with income limits that vary by county Fairfax allows up to 100% relief for incomes under $52,000.

One Virginia catch: localities charge an annual personal property tax on vehicles (the "car tax"), typically $300–$1,500+ per vehicle per year. This is separate from home property tax and is NOT covered by the veteran exemption in most localities.

Virginia first-time homebuyer programs (Virginia Housing)

Virginia Housing (formerly known as VHDA Virginia Housing Development Authority) offers some of the most comprehensive first-time buyer programs in the country. Use our 401(k) calculator to balance saving for the down payment against retirement contributions.

Virginia Housing Down Payment Assistance Grant

Grant of 2% to 2.5% of the home's purchase price applied at closing toward down payment. NOT a loan, NOT repayable. Requires use of a Virginia Housing first-mortgage product and minimum 660 FICO score. Income limits by area apply. Combines with Closing Cost Assistance grant for additional savings.

Plus Second Mortgage

Second mortgage of up to 5% of the home's purchase price to cover down payment and closing costs. Provided as a 30year fixed-rate second loan. Combines with Virginia Housing first mortgage. Useful for buyers needing larger down payment assistance than the grant provides. Income and purchase price limits apply.

Closing Cost Assistance Grant

Grant of up to 2.5% of the loan amount to cover closing costs and prepaid items. Free money — no repayment required. Combines with VA loans, FHA loans, and conventional loans. One of the most popular Virginia Housing offerings since closing costs often surprise first-time buyers ($5,000 to $15,000+ on a typical Virginia home).

Granting Freedom Program (Disabled Veterans)

Grant up to $8,000 for disabled veterans needing home modifications for accessibility wheelchair ramps, widened doorways, accessible bathrooms, lift equipment. Stacks with VA Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) grant for major modifications. Apply through Virginia Housing partner contractors.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the average mortgage rate in Virginia in 2026?
As of June 2026, Virginia 30year fixed mortgage rates average approximately 6.50% near the national average of 6.53%. Northern Virginia sees slightly better rates due to high-income borrowers and strong credit profiles. Virginia credit unions (Navy Federal, Pentagon Federal, Apple Federal) often offer rates 0.15–0.25% below national lenders, especially for military and federal employees. Borrowers with credit scores above 740 typically see additional rate reductions of 0.25–0.5%.
Why is Northern Virginia so much more expensive than the rest of the state?
Northern Virginia is part of the Washington DC metro area, with median home prices ranging $650K–$800K+. The region has the highest concentration of federal government employees, defense contractors, and tech companies (Amazon HQ2 in Arlington, plus Dulles tech corridor) in America. Rest of Virginia is dramatically different — Richmond medians around $325K, Virginia Beach $385K, Roanoke under $250K. If you can work remotely or live outside NOVA, the cost difference is substantial.
How does the VA loan benefit work for military buyers in Virginia?
VA loans require ZERO down payment and ZERO private mortgage insurance (PMI) for eligible veterans, active duty, National Guard, and Reservists. Virginia has the second-highest concentration of military in America (behind California), so VA loans are widely understood by local lenders. The funding fee (typically 2.15–3.3%) can be waived for veterans with service-connected disabilities. Combine VA loan benefits with Virginia Housing first-time buyer assistance for maximum savings.
What is Virginia's disabled veteran property tax exemption?
Virginia provides a FULL property tax exemption on the primary residence of veterans rated 100% permanently and totally disabled by the VA, or surviving spouses of veterans who died from service-connected causes. One of the most generous exemptions in America — saves the entire annual property tax bill, often $4,000–$8,000 per year in Northern Virginia. Apply through your locality's commissioner of revenue. NO income limit applies.
Does Virginia have city or local income tax?
No — Virginia has only state income tax, graduated from 2.0% to 5.75% (top bracket starts at $17,000 of taxable income, making it effectively flat for most middle-class earners). Cities and counties in Virginia do NOT charge separate income tax, unlike Ohio or Pennsylvania. However, Northern Virginia localities charge a personal property tax on vehicles (the "car tax") that can add $300 to $1,500+ per year per vehicle.
What's the best Virginia city for first-time buyers?
Depends on your industry and budget. For maximum affordability: Roanoke ($245K) and Norfolk ($265K) offer real entry points under $300K. For balanced affordability + strong job market: Richmond ($325K) has growing tech, healthcare, and banking employers. For high salaries despite high prices: NOVA — but pair it with VA loan or Virginia Housing programs to manage the price. Virginia Beach ($385K) offers ocean access at moderate prices.

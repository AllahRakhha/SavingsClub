How Does a Paycheck Calculator Work?

A paycheck calculator estimates your take-home pay by subtracting federal income tax, Social Security tax (6.2%), Medicare tax (1.45%), and pre-tax deductions like 401(k) contributions from your gross salary. This calculator uses the 2026 federal tax brackets and standard deduction amounts.

Understanding Your Paycheck Deductions

Your gross salary is not what you take home. Federal income tax is calculated on your adjusted gross income (after standard deduction and pre-tax contributions). Social Security tax applies to the first $168,600 of earnings. Medicare tax applies to all earnings at 1.45%, with an additional 0.9% surcharge above $200,000 for single filers.

How 401(k) Contributions Reduce Your Tax Bill

Pre-tax 401(k) contributions lower your taxable income. If you earn $65,000 and contribute 6% ($3,900), your taxable income drops to $61,100. At the 22% federal bracket, that saves approximately $858 in federal taxes per year. Use our 401(k) Calculator to see how these contributions grow over time.

State Taxes Not Included

This calculator shows federal taxes only. Seven states have no income tax (Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Texas, Washington, Wyoming), while states like California and New York have significant additional state income taxes. Your actual take-home pay will vary by state. Use our Budget Planner to allocate your actual take-home pay effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much of my paycheck goes to taxes?

For a $65,000 salary, approximately 22-28% goes to combined federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare. The exact amount depends on your filing status, deductions, and state taxes.

Why is my paycheck less than expected?

Common reasons: federal and state income taxes, Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), health insurance premiums, 401(k) contributions, and other voluntary deductions. Review your pay stub for a detailed breakdown.

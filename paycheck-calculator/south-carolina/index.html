South Carolina rewrote its income tax in 2026

If you are using a paycheck calculator that still shows South Carolina rates of 0, 3, and 6 percent, it is out of date and your numbers are wrong. On March 30, 2026, Governor McMaster signed H.4216 into law as Act 110, and it changed three things at once.

  • Two brackets replaced three. The rate is now 1.99 percent on South Carolina taxable income under 30,000 dollars, and 5.21 percent minus 966 dollars on income of 30,000 dollars or more.
  • The federal standard deduction no longer applies to your state return. South Carolina formally decoupled from it. Federal adjusted gross income is now the starting point.
  • A new deduction, the SCIAD, replaced it: 15,000 dollars for single filers, 22,500 dollars for head of household, and 30,000 dollars for married filing jointly.

That last one is where the trap is, and almost nobody is talking about it.

The hidden marginal rate that catches middle earners

South Carolina's headline top rate is 5.21 percent. That number is technically true and practically misleading, because the SCIAD does not stay at 15,000 dollars. It shrinks as you earn more.

For a single filer, the deduction starts shrinking once your federal adjusted gross income passes 40,000 dollars, and it hits zero at 95,000 dollars. The statute reduces it by a fraction: the amount your income exceeds 40,000 dollars, divided by 55,000.

Work through what that means. Every extra dollar you earn in that band does two things: it gets taxed, and it shrinks your deduction by about 27 cents, which exposes another 27 cents to tax. So your taxable income actually rises by about 1.27 dollars for every 1 dollar you earn.

The real number. Multiply it out and your true marginal rate between 40,000 and 95,000 dollars is roughly 6.63 percent, not 5.21 percent. We checked it empirically: a single filer earning 75,000 dollars who takes a 1,000 dollar raise pays 66.17 dollars more in South Carolina tax on it. That is 6.62 percent. The headline rate is not the rate you actually face.

Is South Carolina actually cheaper than North Carolina?

South Carolina does win elsewhere. Property taxes are among the lowest in the country, it does not tax Social Security, and it offers a capital gains exclusion that makes it genuinely attractive to investors and retirees. But on a straightforward paycheck, at a middle income, it is not the bargain it is often sold as. What South Carolina does not tax

  • No local or city income tax. Unlike Michigan or Pennsylvania, no South Carolina city takes a bite. Your state tax is the whole state story.
  • No tax on Social Security benefits.
  • Favorable retirement treatment, including a retirement income deduction and preferential handling of military retirement.
Rates may keep falling. Act 110 built in revenue triggers starting in 2027. If income tax collections are projected to grow by at least 5 percent, the top rate must come down. The law says those cuts continue until the top rate reaches 1.99 percent, and then the 1.99 percent itself begins falling toward zero. None of it is guaranteed, because it all depends on the state hitting revenue targets. Track it at the South Carolina Department of Revenue.

How to lower your South Carolina tax

Because of the phase-out, pre-tax contributions are unusually powerful for South Carolina earners in the 40,000 to 95,000 dollar band. A dollar into a traditional 401(k) reduces your federal adjusted gross income, which reduces your state taxable income and claws back part of your SCIAD at the same time. You are getting relief at that 6.63 percent effective rate, not the 5.21 percent headline.

The 2026 employee 401(k) limit is 24,500 dollars. Use our 401(k) calculator to see the effect, and the budget planner to check the contribution actually fits your month.

South Carolina paycheck questions

What is the South Carolina income tax rate for 2026?
South Carolina replaced its old three-bracket system with two brackets under Act 110, signed on March 30, 2026. The rate is 1.99 percent on South Carolina taxable income below 30,000 dollars, and 5.21 percent minus 966 dollars on income of 30,000 dollars or more. The old rates of 0, 3, and 6 percent no longer apply for the 2026 tax year.
What is the SCIAD deduction?
The South Carolina Income Adjusted Deduction replaced the federal standard deduction for state purposes in 2026. It is 15,000 dollars for single and married filing separately, 22,500 dollars for head of household, and 30,000 dollars for married filing jointly. It shrinks as your federal adjusted gross income rises and disappears completely at 95,000 dollars for a single filer.
Why is my real South Carolina marginal tax rate higher than 5.21 percent?
Because the SCIAD deduction phases out as you earn more. For a single filer between 40,000 and 95,000 dollars of income, every extra dollar earned also shrinks the deduction by about 27 cents, so your taxable income rises by roughly 1.27 dollars for every 1 dollar you earn. That pushes the true marginal rate to about 6.63 percent, higher than the headline top rate of 5.21 percent.
How much is taken out of a South Carolina paycheck?
Federal income tax, South Carolina state income tax, Social Security at 6.2 percent up to the 184,500 dollar wage base, and Medicare at 1.45 percent. On a 75,000 dollar salary in 2026, a single filer keeps about 58,935 dollars per year, which is roughly 2,267 dollars every two weeks. There is no local or city income tax anywhere in South Carolina.
Does South Carolina have local or city income taxes?
No. South Carolina has no county or city income tax. Residents pay only the single state income tax on their earnings, which makes the calculation simpler than in states like Michigan or Pennsylvania where local taxes apply on top.
Is South Carolina cheaper than North Carolina for taxes?
Not at a typical salary, which surprises most people. On 75,000 dollars, a single filer pays about 2,657 dollars in South Carolina against about 2,484 dollars in North Carolina, so South Carolina costs roughly 173 dollars more. South Carolina does have advantages elsewhere, including lower property taxes and a capital gains exclusion, but on ordinary wage income North Carolina is slightly cheaper at this level.
Will South Carolina income tax go down in the future?
It may. Act 110 includes revenue triggers starting in 2027. If individual income tax collections are projected to grow by at least 5 percent, the top rate must be reduced. The law says these cuts continue until the top rate falls to 1.99 percent, and then the 1.99 percent rate itself begins falling toward zero. None of that is guaranteed, because it depends on state revenue growth.
Does South Carolina tax Social Security or retirement income?
South Carolina does not tax Social Security benefits. It does tax other retirement income such as pensions and 401k withdrawals, though the state offers a retirement income deduction that reduces the amount subject to tax. Military retirement also receives favorable treatment. This calculator covers wage income, not retirement distributions.

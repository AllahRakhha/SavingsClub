Understanding Mortgages in Texas

Texas is one of the most active housing markets in the United States, with strong population growth, a robust economy, and home prices that remain affordable compared to coastal states. However, Texas homebuyers face a unique financial tradeoff: while the state has no income tax, it relies heavily on property tax revenue, resulting in some of the highest property tax bills in the country. Understanding this tradeoff is essential before you buy.

The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Texas averages approximately 6.45%, slightly below the national average of 6.53%. Texas mortgage rates tend to track close to national rates because the state's economy is large and stable, attracting major national lenders that offer competitive pricing.

Texas Property Tax: What You Need to Know

With an effective property tax rate of 1.58%, Texas ranks among the top 10 highest property tax states in America. For a median-priced Texas home of $310,000, that means approximately $4,900 per year in property taxes alone — or roughly $408 per month added to your mortgage payment.

Texas does offer a homestead exemption that reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by $100,000 for school district taxes, and additional exemptions exist for seniors (age 65+) and disabled veterans. Always apply for your homestead exemption — it can save you $1,000-$3,000 per year.

Median Home Prices Across Texas

The statewide median home price in Texas sits around $310,000, but prices vary dramatically by city. Texas saw home prices grow approximately 3-4% in 2025, slower than the national average — partly because of the state's significant new home construction. Texas builds more new homes than any other state, which keeps prices in check during periods of high demand.

Texas First-Time Homebuyer Programs

Texas offers several programs that can dramatically reduce upfront costs for qualified first-time buyers:

My First Texas Home (TDHCA)

Down payment and closing cost assistance up to 5% of the loan amount, plus competitive 30-year fixed rates. Income limits apply (typically $95,000-$130,000 depending on county and household size).

Home Sweet Texas Home Loan (TSAHC)

Grants of 3-5% of the mortgage amount that do NOT require repayment. Available to teachers, police, firefighters, EMS, and veterans without first-time buyer restrictions.

Texas Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)

A federal tax credit worth up to $2,000 per year for the life of your loan. Reduces your federal income tax bill directly — not just a deduction.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average mortgage rate in Texas?
As of May 2026, Texas mortgage rates average approximately 6.45% for a 30-year fixed loan, slightly below the national average of 6.53%. Rates vary by lender, credit score, and down payment — borrowers with credit scores above 740 typically see rates 0.25-0.5% lower than the average.
Why are Texas property taxes so high?
Texas has no state income tax, so the state and local governments rely heavily on property taxes to fund schools, roads, police, and other public services. The tradeoff: you keep more of your paycheck but pay more on your home. For most homeowners, the math still favors Texas compared to high-income-tax states like California or New York.
How much income do I need to afford a Texas home?
For a median-priced $310,000 Texas home with 10% down, you typically need a gross household income of $75,000-$85,000 to keep total housing costs (PITI) under 28% of income. For higher-cost areas like Austin ($540,000 median), you'd need closer to $130,000-$145,000.
What is the Texas homestead exemption?
The Texas homestead exemption reduces the taxable value of your primary residence by $100,000 for school district property taxes. Additional exemptions apply for seniors 65+, disabled persons, and disabled veterans (some are fully exempt). You must apply with your county appraisal district — it's not automatic.
Should I buy in Austin, Dallas, or Houston?
Each market has tradeoffs. Austin has the highest home prices ($540K median) but strong tech-driven job growth. Dallas-Fort Worth ($425K) offers the most diverse economy and largest job market. Houston ($340K) is the most affordable major Texas metro and has the largest energy and medical sectors. Property tax rates are similar across all three (1.93%-2.16%). Your decision should be driven primarily by job opportunity and lifestyle preference, not price alone.
